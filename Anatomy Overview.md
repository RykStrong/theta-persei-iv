## "Nominal" Body Plan

### Most Common Configuration

**Basic Pattern:**
- **Body:** Flattened oval
- **Girdle:** [Hexagirdle or octagirdle](Hexagirdle%20Octagirdle.md) (ring-shaped hip/shoulder)
- **Legs:** 6-8 (hexapod most common)
- **Nervous system:** Central loop through girdle
- **Brain:** Protected in body center
- **Lungs:** Many small, with [spiracles](Respiratory%20Systems.md)
- **Hearts:** Many small, distributed
- **Sensors:** On flexible stalks above brain
- **Gut:** Through-gut (mouth → anus)
- **Mouth aids:** Pedipalps or trunks, or mouths at ends of trunks

---
## Variation From Pattern

**Different Configurations:**
- [Aerial organisms](Flying%20Forest.md): Gas bladders, wing structures
- [Aquatic forms](Ecology%20Overview.md): Fin arrangements, streamlined bodies
- [Distributed Organisms](Distributed%20Organisms%20Overview.md): Multiple separated bodies
- Small organisms: Simplified structures
- Autotrophs: Sessile or mobile plant-analogs

**No True Homogeneity:**
There is no true homogeneity of gross anatomy among life-forms. Each partifunct's organs have their own blood circulation. Some organisms have a partifunct dedicated to blood/circulation, others do not.

### Distributed Systems

**Multiple Small Units vs Single Large:**
- **Hearts:** Many small hearts throughout body (not one large heart)
- **Lungs:** Many small lungs with spiracles (not two large lungs)
- **Brains:** Multiple specialized [brain modules](Nervous%20Systems.md) (not unified brain)

**Advantages:**
- Resistant to circulatory collapse
- Harder to bleed to death
- Redundancy = resilience
- One damaged system ≠ death

---

### Partifunct Integration

**Physical Connections:**
- **Skeletal:** Ball-and-socket joints, tendon attachments
- **Vascular:** [Capillary exchange organs](Circulatory%20Systems.md)
- **Neural:** Nerve bundle crossings
- **Muscle:** Tendons span partifunct boundaries

**Example (from source):**
The legs ST-LMB partifunct's bone ends in a ball which fits into a socket on the hexagirdle, the muscles of the leg partifunct have a tendon that attaches to the girdle, and muscles of the girdle attach via tendon to the leg bone.

---
### Shared Organs

**Interface Structures:**
Shared organs, where the capillaries of two partifuncts infiltrate to within a single membrane of each other, are used to:
- Pass blood sugar
- Clean blood
- Spread hormones

These [transfer organs](Circulatory%20Systems.md) enable metabolic coordination without genetic fusion.

## Tissue Architecture

### BLU-Based Construction

**Not Cellular:**
- [Basic Life Units](BLU%20Biology.md) (100× larger than cells)
- [Fungal-like continuous tissue](BLU%20Tissue.md)
- [Genode clusters](GFP%20and%20Genodules.md) act as nuclei analogs
- Cytoplasmic streaming between regions

**Tissue Types:**
- **High-density genode clusters:** Active tissues (muscle, neural, digestive)
- **Low-density genode clusters:** Structural tissues (bone, connective)
- **Interface zones:** Specialized at partifunct boundaries

## Common Anatomical Observations

### Spiracles and Breathing

**Respiratory Features:**
Frequently, blood is pumped using many small heart partifuncts and oxygenated via many small lung partifuncts. Such creatures are covered with stomata openings for air-based oxygen/carbon dioxide exchange.

**Advantage:**
- Distributed gas exchange
- Efficient with 25% O₂ atmosphere
- Difficult to suffocate
- Surface area maximized

---

### Joint Architecture

**Swollen Joints:**
Some creatures have joints that are separate partifuncts from the limbs, giving a 'swollen joint' appearance to many limbed organisms.

**Structure:**
- Joints as distinct partifuncts (in some organisms)
- Or specialized tissue at limb segment boundaries
-  Gliding joints and other special configurations

---

### Skeletal Materials

**Two Primary Types:**

**Lignin-Based (75%):**
- [Lignin](Skeletal%20Systems.md) or similar proteins
- Lighter than calcium
- Flexible when thin
- Very strong when thick

**Calcium-Based (25%):**
- Similar to Earth bone
- Heavier but more rigid
- Used in some specialized structures

## Body Plans Across Biomes

### Terrestrial

**Dominant Pattern:**
- Hexapod or octapod walkers
- [Hexagirdle](Hexagirdle%20Octagirdle.md) most common
- Variable size (mouse to elephant analogs)
- Grazers, browsers, predators

---

### Aerial

**Lighter Construction:**
- Gas bladders (hydrogen/helium from atmosphere)
- Thin bones, reduced mass
- [Flying Forest](Flying%20Forest.md) specialists
- Some use wings, others pure buoyancy

---

### Aquatic

**Streamlined Forms:**
- Fins instead of legs
- Gills or specialized lungs
- Variable body plans
- [Marine diversity](Ecology%20Overview.md) high

---

### Autotroph ("Plant")

**Sessile or Mobile:**
- Some rooted, some walk slowly
- [Wandering forms](Ecology%20Overview.md) exist
- Photosynthetic organs
- Variable structures

## Convergent Evolution

### Earth-Like Solutions

The physiology of the life forms on TPIV is constrained by the environment, and the earth-like environment has produced very many life forms that bear gross similarity to forms on earth, both in appearance and behavior.

**Similar Niches:**
- Grazers resemble ungulates
- Predators resemble carnivores
- Flyers resemble birds/bats

**Different Implementations:**
- Multiple genetic lineages in one body
- [Context-dependent expression](Multi%20Expression%20Potential.md)
- No fixed species
- Rapid evolutionary innovation

- Many organisms omnivorous
