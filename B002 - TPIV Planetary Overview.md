## Hydrosphere & Oceanography

- **Ocean Coverage:** ~65%
- **Salinity Gradient:** Highly variable due to interconnected inland seas and lunar pumping effects
- **Tidal Forces:** Complex, driven primarily by fast-orbiting Tida (1.5 tides per day, 10–15 m baseline); modulated by Lumna on 7-day cycle (spring/neap tides); chaotic super-tides (18–25 m) occur unpredictably every 85–90 days due to Radita's incommensurate orbital contribution; compound effects cause massive flooding in tidal basins during super-tide events
- **Meteoric Input:** Organic-rich impactors frequently deliver material to ocean basins; elevated metal silicate content observed in shallow coastlines

## Lithosphere & Geology

**Crust Composition:**
- Predominantly silicate-based, with high regional concentration of calcium-magnesium compounds
- Unusually low concentrations of heavy metals (Fe, Ni, Cu), leading to biologically driven metal accumulation strategies in flora

**Plate Activity:** Moderate — Magna shows long cratonic stability; active rift zones on Kolofluke and Apanon

**Volcanism:** Active zones on 3 major islands and 2 subduction arcs; frequently influences atmospheric particulate patterns

---

## Climate Systems

**Circulation Pattern:**
- Dominated by three major Hadley cells per hemisphere, with unusual persistence of equatorial storm tracks
- Jet streams vary seasonally due to planetary rotation and tidal resonance with Tida

**Cloud Formation:**
- Cirrus-like high-altitude sulfur-ice clouds noted in upper tropics
- Dense fog and low-lying condensation layers in coastal temperate zones

**Seasonal Patterns:**
- Mild seasonal change across most latitudes
- Tidal cycles and moon-phase compound effects introduce complex sub-seasonal rhythms (see [B012 – Composite Life Cycles.md](B012%20%E2%80%93%20Composite%20Life%20Cycles.md))

## Biological Zonation

Automated and visual survey reveals biomes conforming to both altitudinal and lunar-influenced longitudinal banding:

- **Steppe Biomes:** Dominant on Magna; large herds, predation-specialised taxa, minimal canopy cover
- **Flying Forests:** Mobile high-atmosphere autotroph aggregates; variable structure and mass
- **Salt Flats & Tidal Zones:** High biodiversity and observed partifunct turnover; likely evolutionary hotspot
- **Seafloor Lattices:** Preliminary sonar from probe Odysseus-2 indicates complex undersea structures — awaiting confirmation

## Land Masses

### Magna
Very large continent spanning many biomes, straddles equator and reaches 70°N and 35°S. The largest continent, spanning from the northern polar region (one peninsula) to the mid-latitudes of the southern hemisphere (two large areas). Features massive mountain ranges along its eastern and southwestern coasts and vast inland plains straddling the equator. Contains a central depression forming a massive seasonal lake system of tidally connected endorheic basins. The predominantly flat area north of the equator hosts vast grasslands and the greatest diversity of grazer composite organisms. Home to the greatest biodiversity due to varied climate zones.

### Dualis
Southern hemisphere, two large subcontinents connected by a narrow isthmus. The northern portion has large tropical islands while the southern sub-continent, with many lakes, extends partly into Antarctic latitudes. Touches Magna in its northern section. The connection point creates a unique mixing zone for partifuncts.

### Piedmont
Smaller continent in the northern hemisphere, adjacent to Magna. Low rolling hills, remains of ancient mountains.

### Apanon
Round continent with tall mountains forming a half circle in the center. Distributed Organisms are the apex predators, using insect-like inter-communicators.

### Kolo & Fluke
Mountains run along the central spine of Kolo (similar to Madagascar). The very deep valleys host the [Dark Forest](Dark%20Forest%20Kolofluke.md). Distributed Organisms are the apex, using radio waves for inter-communication.

## Notes on Bio-Ecological Anomalies

1. Flora coloration shows deviation from known photosynthetic pigments (see [B003 – TPIV Orbital Surveys.md](B003%20%E2%80%93%20TPIV%20Orbital%20Surveys.md)); near-IR and violet dominant
2. Organism morphology appears highly modular; later dispatches suggest assemblage from independent reproductive units
3. Low cellular clarity — initial microscopy shows tissue masses not clearly bounded by traditional cells, but structured by BLUs (Basic Life Units)
4. Ecosystem pulse synchronisation has been correlated with Radita's 1.3-day orbital period and 6.4-hour EM pulse cycle
