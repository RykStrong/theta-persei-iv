# B003 – TPIV Orbital Surveys

## Summary

Initial surface scans and orbital reflectance data from both pre-crew automated satellites and the first synchronous polar orbits of GH-TPIV-B1 have confirmed a planet-wide deviation from Earth and NM3 photosynthetic coloration norms. TPIV flora exhibit no dominant pigment band, with diverse autotrophic clades absorbing across multiple, irregularly spaced wavelength ranges.

Autotrophic coloration clusters are visually and spectrally distinct across latitudes and biomes, including several unique forms optimised for the UV-rich and red-attenuated light environment of Theta Persei. Particularly striking is the coexistence of multiple primary pigment strategies within a single biome, suggesting an absence of monopolising photosynthetic pathways.

This chromatic mosaicism may serve multiple roles, including:
- Passive UV filtering
- Predator confusion or mimicry camouflage
- Modular spectral sharing in composite autotrophic surfaces

---

## Findings

### Dominant Pigment Reflection Peaks

- 510 nm (blue-green edge)
- 600 nm (orange-red)
- 740 nm (near-IR bandpass)
- Notably absent: strong reflection in central green (~550 nm)

### Visual Groupings of Autotroph Types

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| "Magenta Canopy" | Broad-leafed, high-altitude forest analogs, dominant in temperate valleys |
| "Obsidian Flatleaf" | Low-lying mat-formers; found in arid uplands and basins |
| "Amber Planktoforms" | High-reflective, free-floating organisms over open inland seas |
| "Blue Threadmoss" | Dense filamentous colonies in tidal surge zones; extremely UV-resistant |

---

## Biological Interpretation

The current working hypothesis is that these pigments (tentatively named **Glyrochromes**) evolved under dual pressures:

1. Adaptation to Theta Persei's strong UV output, which is more pronounced than Sol's spectrum
2. Modular photonic stacking, wherein surface autotrophs exchange pigmentary layers or align reflectance phases with neighbours

The diversity of light-harvesting strategies may not imply evolutionary competition but organisms dynamically combining layered photoelements to optimise local conditions.

---

## Implications

- **Photosynthetic Spectral Range Extension:** Organisms may be capturing energy in bands rarely utilised by Earth-based life, increasing theoretical biomass efficiency.
- **Visual Camouflage and Communication:** The overlapping ranges of reflection and absorption may also serve intra-species signalling or ecosystem-level light manipulation.
- **Thermal Regulation:** Some Glyrochromes appear to radiate in IR bands, potentially as a cooling mechanism.

---

## Recommendations

- Drone swarms should be fitted with multi-band hyperspectral cameras including high-UV and deep-NIR capability.
- Autotroph tissue samples for Glyrochrome extraction and isolation are priority for ground team Alpha.
