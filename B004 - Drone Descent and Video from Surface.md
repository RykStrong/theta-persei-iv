# B004 – Drone Descent & Video from Surface

## Summary

Today we completed the first full descent of Prospector 1, flying over the western section of the Magna Steppe. The terrain rolls gently — mostly scrub and brush — but broken in places by mesas and salt cuts, with long blue stream ribbons and isolated plateaus topped with active violet canopy. From the air, the flora never sits still. Large surface structures shimmer and tilt with clear motor behaviour. Broad leafmats ripple, and root structures flex at angles that suggest deliberate shifting. We are not looking at passive growth. These "plants" move, sometimes in locomotion, more often with rhythmic contractile behaviour.

But it's the animals that have shaken us the most. We see dozens of distinct morphologies, ranging from squat grazers in herds to long-limbed, bounding figures near the canyon edges. They vary in size, ornamentation, coloration, and limb proportion. And yet — beneath the chaos — shared anatomy keeps surfacing. Six legs or eight legs, forward-set eating structures, bilateral torso flares.

We've coined the term "MSV" — Morpho-Structural Variant — to start tracking these shapes. We believe these may not represent fully distinct species, but rather differently expressed variants of a common body plan, perhaps similar to 'castes' among eusocial insects on Earth.

## Observations

### Hexapod Norm
A clear majority (approx. 70%) of the mobile fauna are hexapodal. The limb joints vary, but most feature a triple-joint (shoulder, knee, ankle) configuration consistent with hinge and ball-like structures.

### Non-Homogeneity of Form
Every large animal-like figure is morphologically distinct, and yet shares repeating body modules: consistent flank spacing, similar respiratory vent placements, limb socket arrays in mirrored alignment.

### Respiration Systems
Multiple subjects exhibited rhythmic expansion at lateral body segments, likely representing modular lungs or stomatal membranes. At least one tri-lobed grazer displayed sequential pulse patterns suggesting distributed respiration.

### Plant Activity
Surface flora show high degrees of responsiveness — tilting toward drone shadow, vibrating in response to wind pressure, or curling in anticipation of nearby mobile fauna. Some root mats pull free and slowly drift across the topsoil, churning as they go.

### Sample Retrieval
Prospector 1 deployed two sample darts in the central plains and outer mesa scrub zone. Samples include airborne particulates, a partial detached lung membrane, and flexible root-fibre from the motile "clam grasses." Atmospheric filter returns show a complex biological load, with significant GFP presence in both upper-air and low-drift samples. Initial analysis in [B004.B01 – Aerial Sample Analysis.md](B004.B01%20%E2%80%93%20Aerial%20Sample%20Analysis.md).
