# B005 – Steppe Surface Life & Initial Morphology Notes

## Summary

As surface drone missions expand across the central steppe basin of TPIV's equatorial belt, we are now able to provide a tentative baseline for visible biota, locomotion types, external structure patterns, and ecological behaviours.

This marks a notable turning point in the xenobiological record: the surface life observed across a wide geographic area shares consistent morphological layouts, but those layouts are realised through diverse body plans, suggesting modular inheritance without tight lineage constraints. The researchers have coined the term **Morphological Stability Vector (MSV)** to describe the tendency of unrelated composite organisms to converge on similar overall body shapes and behaviours.

Most large surface organisms fall within a hexapodal template — but limb layout, material, and visible sensory apparatuses vary significantly even among organisms in the same visible niche.

Simultaneously, plant-analogues display a far greater degree of movement and responsiveness than terrestrial flora. While some rooted autotrophs remain relatively fixed, most exhibit slow creeping, vertical adjustment, or canopy rotation behaviour. Many of these are not sessile and are capable of relocation, and in one case a low shrub appeared to rapidly collapse and re-expand its foliage in response to overhead shadow.

## Visual Survey: Steppe Biome (Region 18.3°N – 117.9°E)

**Dominant Grazers:**
- Zebra Grazer
- Ringback Broadbeast
- Bristle-Spined Forager

**Predators:**
- Banded Pack Hunter
- Duskmantle Stalker
- Drophunter Complex

**Autotrophs:**
- Stalk Trees
- Clam Grasses
- Brownflower Shrubs

Drone footage and retrievals confirm gross anatomical diversity, but often conforming to repeated themes: sensory fronds, bilateral jaws, dorsal respiratory slots, and 6–8 limb base templates. A few observed grazers appear to host parasite-like structures that exhibit limited independence.

## Genetic & Tissue Reference

Collected biological samples (via Prospector 2) show consistent BLU-based construction — a fibrous tissue lattice lacking cellular membranes, but exhibiting regenerative properties. Tissues mimic conventional organs: muscle, connective tissue, absorptive surfaces.

---

## Flying Forest Reference

The Flying Forest remains in low seasonal drift just above the steppe zone, casting irregular moving shadows. Its presence influences behavioural responses from many surface organisms, possibly due to shading, drifting GFP rain, or gravitational/auditory cues.
