# B005.B01 – Dissection of Herd Foragers: Structural Notes

## Summary

This dispatch details the gross dissection of a set of 10 medium-bodied foragers retrieved from the Magna steppe. The subjects — six- and eight-legged terrestrial organisms — were collected via drone for dissection and analysis.

---

## Core Findings

### 1. General Gross Anatomical Similarity

All individuals had gross bilateral symmetry, one or two front mouth stalks, six- or eight-sided hip/shoulder girdle (hexagirdle/octagirdle ring), six or eight legs in pairs, a through-digestive tract with a rear anus, a womb organ (**note: ALL specimens had wombs**), a central top sensory stalk with eyes, ears, and nose, a concentration of 'brain' tissue in a protected area inside the girdle, and a circulatory system (or two).

**Baseline features across all specimens:**
- Bilateral symmetry
- One or two frontal oral stalks terminating in a hardened mouth cone
- A shoulder/hip girdle
- Paired limb sets with robust musculature and compact hinge structures
- Broad stripes on the flanks
- A complete digestive tract with food intake and waste ejection clearly differentiated
- A central top sensory stalk bearing clustered visual, chemical, and vibratory sensory tissues
- A protective internal cavity beneath the girdle housing a soft central processing tissue ("brain analog")
- Circulatory systems — all specimens had at least one; most had two distinct but cooperative systems
- Womb structures present in every specimen regardless of external sex markers

**Body metrics:** Average weight ~500 kg. Footpad to dorsal plate ~1.2 m; sensory stalk adding ~1 m. Overall length 2.4–3.1 m depending on gait anatomy.

### 2. Non-Homogeneity

Despite behavioural uniformity, gross anatomical non-homogeneity was pronounced:
- 4 individuals: single integrated circulatory system
- 6 individuals: multiple systems (one for oxygen/nutrient delivery; one for waste, metabolism, and internal sensory regulation)
- 3 individuals: eight legs; 7 individuals: six legs
- Spiracle configurations varied significantly:
  - Some featured wing-like covers for environmental shielding
  - Others had feather-clustered spiracle arrays (possibly for passive filtration or thermoregulation)

### 3. Limb Number Variations

While six- and eight-legged individuals were dissected, four-legged individuals were also observed in the field.

### 4. Compartmentalisation of Anatomy

The circulatory system has a "transfer organ" at each limb segment and each organ; each organ has its own inner circulatory system that connects to the 'mains' via this transfer organ. Spiracles lead to trachea, which lead to many small lungs. Legs again seem to have separate air systems.

### 5. Organ Interface Elements

Organs frequently have interface elements and separate internal blood flow.
