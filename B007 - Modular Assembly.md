# B007 – Modular Assembly, Environmental Reproduction, and Biological Convergence

## Summary

Ongoing dissections and tissue mapping have confirmed that composite TPIV organisms are constructed from discrete, genetically distinct biological units — each representing a functional subsystem that is not genetically identical to the others.

These modular units, now designated **Partifuncts** (from the French *partie fonctionnelle*), exhibit:
- Independent genomic bundles (GFP sets)
- Semi-autonomous metabolic cycles
- Physical and chemical boundaries distinct from neighbouring systems

The emerging model suggests that TPIV organisms are not single genetic individuals. Rather, they are cooperative assemblies of multiple, evolutionarily distinct units.

---

## Observational Highlights

- GFP analysis from dissection samples reveals non-redundant, complementary expression domains
  - Limb tissues, digestive tracts, and respiratory systems all carry unique, non-overlapping GFP sets
- MGFP-C and MGFP-G proteins are unique to each subsystem and do not cross domains
- Histological boundaries between modules are sharply defined — with no intermediate gradients
- Vascular systems and signal pathways respect these borders, communicating via shared interfaces only

---

## Terminology & Formal Definition

### Partifunct *(n.)*

> A genetically distinct, modular biological subsystem that expresses a specific functional domain within a composite organism, retaining independent genomic identity and metabolic architecture.

- Not equivalent to an Earth organ
- Not equivalent to an Earth zooid
- Closer to a self-replicating "organ" that can integrate into and function within larger composites — imagine your organs each had their own DNA

## Foundational Concepts

### BLU Tissue
All TPIV organisms are constructed from Basic Life Units (BLUs) — tissue-scale biological elements analogous to Earth cells, but significantly larger and genetically partial. Each BLU performs specialised functions and depends on the larger structure for viability.

### Modular Physiology
BLUs are expressed in partifuncts, which perform broad anatomical and metabolic roles within the composite body. Partifuncts operate semi-independently and are genetically distinct from one another. Partifuncts grow together into larger composite organisms.

### Environmental GFP Accumulation
Adult organisms continuously shed and collect environmental GFP (eGFP) — Genomic Folded Proteins released by other organisms into the local biome. Most eGFPs are inert nutrients, but a subset are reproductive carriers capable of integrating with compatible internal systems.

### Multi-Expression Potential of GFP
Identical GFP sequences can express differently depending on their composite context. The same Genomic Folded Protein (GFP) may give rise to distinct BLU expressions across different organisms or environments. For example, a GFP lineage responsible for limb articulation in a terrestrial grazer may express as:
- A segmented leg in a mobile grazer
- A bending stalk in a rooted autotroph
- A flexible branch in a flying forest canopy organism

This suggests that expression is epigenetically or chemically modulated by the surrounding composite structure. The phenotypic result is determined not solely by the GFP content, but by its structural context, neighbouring BLUs, and developmental conditions within the womb partifunct or environmental assembly site.

This capability implies:
- Extreme reusability of core GFP libraries across ecological roles
- Shared ancestry between apparently unrelated taxa (e.g., flora and fauna)
- The possibility of modular phenotypic switching under certain environmental or gestational conditions

### Genetic Integration via Partifzygotes
Each partifunct recombines its internal GFP with collected eGFP, forming a **partifzygote**. These are transferred to a dedicated womb partifunct, where embryonic assembly occurs.

### Assembly-Conception Process
In contrast to Earth-like fertilisation, TPIV reproduction involves modular assembly of viable partifzygotes:
- Compatible units are recruited and spatially organised
- Epigenetic patterning cues exchange between modules
- The womb partifunct guides composite body plan formation
- Embryos detach only once metabolic and structural integration is complete

---

## Ecological and Evolutionary Implications

- Offspring do not resemble parents closely — species identity is preserved, but fine-grained traits vary
- Rapid phenotypic shifts occur in response to ecosystem-level changes in eGFP distribution
- Evolution acts at multiple levels:
  - Composite organism fitness
  - Individual partifunct fitness
  - eGFP distribution success across the biome

---

## Distinguishing Traits

This biological system enables:
- Conservative continuity via stable BLU and partifunct associations
- Lottery-style innovation via modular recombination
- Environmental reproduction, where gene viability depends on ambient availability and compatibility
