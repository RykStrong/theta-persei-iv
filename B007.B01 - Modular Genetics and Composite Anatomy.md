# B007.B01 – Modular Genetics and Composite Anatomy

## Summary

Field dissection, drone footage, and molecular analysis converge toward a radical conclusion: TPIV organisms are modular biological composites built from independent genetic units. We introduce the term **partifunct** to describe these genetically distinct, functional modules.

A partifunct is a self-contained genetic subsystem capable of expressing as a physical part in various composite organisms. Overlapping usage across unrelated composites and evidence of independent evolutionary pressures confirms the partifunct as the core biological unit of TPIV.

An adult organism is a set of partifuncts working together, tightly integrated into a single organism.

The reproductive process has a gene-recombining feature; it is not sexual in the traditional sense. The choice of which eGFP to recombine with is probabilistic, governed by hormonal triggers, compatibility signalling, and the ecological availability of viable partifunct carriers. Specialised structures allow internal partifuncts to shed and gather eGFP (silk-like tubules or pores). eGFP may enter the reproductive system not only via consumption but also by environmental uptake, vector deposition, or direct contact. eGFP are normally encapsulated in some BLU tissue, which takes many forms.

## Mechanism of Reproduction and Assembly

### Environmental GFP Acquisition
- Adult composite organisms actively or passively gather GFP packages from the environment
- These include air-carried motes, aquatic dispersals, pollen-like vectors, and genetic carriers such as mites and mobile droplets
- The collected pool is known as eGFP (Environmental GFP)

### Self-Matching and Genetic Exchange
- Each partifunct collects one or more matching copies of its own GFP lineage (sGFP) from the eGFP pool
- It then conducts genetic exchange using sGFP and compatible eGFP, resulting in a **partifzygote**
- This process occurs independently in each partifunct and varies across individuals and circumstances

### Womb-Assisted Genetic Exchange
- Each partifunct sends a sGFP bundle to the womb
- The womb selects a eGFP bundle
- Womb-assisted recombination produces a partifzygote

### Transfer to Womb and Assembly-Conception
- Partifzygotes are transferred to a womb partifunct — a specialised biological subsystem responsible for organising reproduction
- Within the womb structure, **assembly-conception** occurs — the moment when all collected partifzygotes are synchronised and cooperatively merged into a developing composite embryo
- The process initiates gestation, which may result in live birth, external gestational encystment, or egg-laying depending on host configuration

The concept of a "single organism" is emergent. Functional integration, evolutionary fitness, and inheritance operate across modular, distributed contributors.

## BLU vs. Cellular Biology

- The tissues of TPIV organisms are composed of Basic Life Units (BLUs) rather than traditional cells
- BLUs are approximately 10 to 100× larger than Earth cells and typically do not contain the full genome
- Despite this, tissue formed from BLUs is visually and structurally indistinguishable from Earth's cellular tissue
- BLUs appear in repeating patterns dictated by the originating partifunct's developmental instructions

## Conceptual Framework

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Partifunct | A genetically distinct, modular biological unit |
| Composite Organism | A single creature made up of partifuncts that have grown together |
| Partifzygote | A recombined genetic package ready for assembly; only exists as part of the reproductive cycle |
| Womb Partifunct | The internal structure managing organismal assembly |
| Assembly-Conception | The point where an organism's distinct partifzygotes are coordinated into a single viable structure |
| eGFP | Environmental GFP, gathered by organisms to power the reproductive cycle |
| BLU | Basic Life Unit — the large, modular analog to Earth cells |
| Taxon | The rough equivalent of a species in TPIV biology. No genetic code specifies which partifuncts make up a composite organism. |

> *Note: This framework marks a major shift in our understanding of alien life. Subsequent dispatches will classify partifuncts not only by function but by recombination frequency, ecological prevalence, and gestational behaviour.*
