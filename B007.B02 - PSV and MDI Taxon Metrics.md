# B007.B02 – Partifunct Classifications and PSV/MDI Systems for Taxon Modelling

## Summary

This document establishes the formal metrics used to analyse, compare, and classify modular composite organisms observed on TPIV. Two quantitative frameworks are proposed: **Partifunct Set Variance (PSV)** and **Morphological Divergence Index (MDI)**.

---

## Framework 1: Partifunct Set Variance (PSV)

**Definition:** PSV is the percentage of sampled individuals in a given taxon or population that share the same core set of partifuncts (± one standard deviation).

**Purpose:** To determine how structurally consistent a given modular organism population is. A high PSV suggests evolutionary convergence or reproductive filtering toward a consistent form. A low PSV indicates broader environmental sampling, lower developmental fidelity, or ecological plasticity.

**Formula (Simplified):**
```
PSV = (n_shared ±1σ) / n_total × 100
```

**Application Example:**
> "PSV of Savannah Bison Grazer (LGW-Hx-2M) = 84.6%"
> → 84.6% of sampled individuals had the same partifunct configuration within a standard deviation of variance.

**Interpretive Thresholds:**

| PSV | Interpretation |
|-----|----------------|
| >90% | High internal fidelity; likely strong reproductive filtering or womb-controlled assembly |
| 60–89% | Moderate uniformity; adaptive variability |
| <60% | High modular plasticity; open ecological niche construction |

---

## Framework 2: Morphological Divergence Index (MDI)

**Definition:** MDI is a numerical rating from 0 to 10+ that quantifies how morphologically divergent individuals are within a population that shares a core partifunct set. It measures visual and anatomical variability — not merely internal structure.

**Purpose:** To distinguish how visibly different modular individuals appear, even when structurally similar on the partifunct level.

**Interpretive Guide:**

| MDI | Interpretation |
|-----|----------------|
| <2 | Homogeneous forms; low expression variability |
| 2–5 | Body plans diverge, but retain recognisable silhouette |
| >5 | Widely diverse forms with shared functional loadouts |

**Application Example:**
> "MDI of Zebra Grazer Group = 4.8"
> → Individuals differ in limb number, head structure, sensor configuration, coloration, and respiratory pore placement.

**Remarks:**
- High MDI with high PSV implies controlled modularity with divergent expression
- High MDI with low PSV suggests environmental assembly-conception or broad eGFP capture
