# B007a – Modular Entities on TPIV: Exovants vs. Partivants

## Summary

Two distinct biological modalities have emerged from the ongoing analysis of xenocomposite lifeforms: Separated Behavior modules (**Exovants**) and Distributed Organism nodes (**Partivants**). While both derive from the flexible partifunct foundation of TPIV life, they diverge sharply in morphology, lifecycle, integration, and autonomy.

---

## Definitions

### Exovant
A temporary, semi-autonomous unit deployed by a host organism during Separated Behavior (SB). Exovants are composed of multiple partifuncts arranged for a specific task and are metabolically dependent on reintegration with the parent organism.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Duration: Operational window typically <4 hours
- Functionality: Specialised (e.g., hunting, scouting, manipulating)
- Anatomy: Complex, often including sensory and motor systems
- Reintegration: Reattaches to host to replenish glucose, process waste, and reset internal biochemistry
- Persistence: Long-lived and reusable; not expendable

**Example Taxa Using Exovants:**
- Drophunter Blimp (kill units = exovants)
- Throwjaw
- Tossclaw
- Flyeye (used for aerial scouting)

---

### Partivant
A permanently discrete node of a Distributed Organism (DO), composed of one or more partifuncts. Partivants perform continuous, specialised tasks and interact via dense chemical or electromagnetic communication.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Autonomy: Each node is physiologically distinct and capable of sustained independent activity
- Integration: Systemic cohesion via hormonal, pheromonal, or RF signalling (typically through fast-moving DO-mites)
- Role Specialisation: Highly divergent between reproductive, locomotor, sensory, digestive, or cognitive functions
- Lifespan: Persistent throughout organismal lifetime; loss of key nodes leads to systemic collapse

**Example Distributed Organisms:**
- Horse Do-Grazer (herbivorous collective with reproductive specialisation)
- River Guardian (filter-feeding aquatic DO)
- Weaver Grass (semi-sessile DO with buried cognitive partivant nodes)

## Comparison

| Feature | Exovant | Partivant |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Integration Style | Temporary, plug-in | Permanent, decentralised |
| Origin | Internal assembly | Independent or epigenetic origin |
| Autonomy | Limited, time-bound | Sustained |
| Specialisation | Task-specific | Role-specific |
| Communication | Host-organism centric | Inter-node swarms (DO-mites) |
