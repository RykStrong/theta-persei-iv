## Summary

The Drophunter is an apex predator of the Magna steppe ecosystem, notable for its cooperative feeding behaviour between a lighter-than-air carrier organism and its modular exovant hunter-pack. These exovants are not disposable. Rather, they are persistent, metabolically dependent subsystems that reattach to the blimp for sustenance and waste cycling.

---

## Behavioural Pattern

**Operational State:**
The Drophunter blimp floats at mid-altitudes, scanning for herds. Its attached exovants travel with it, physically plugged into vascular sockets beneath the envelope, enabling glucose recharging and waste removal.

**Deployment:**
Upon sighting prey, the blimp descends slightly and releases 6–12 exovants via controlled glide. These exovants coordinate on the ground using scent trails and chemical signalling.

**Engagement:**
- Prey is pursued and brought down via coordinated pack tactics
- Exovants use front-mounted dog-like jaw appendages for biting, disabling, and eventually butchering the kill
- Exovants chew the meat while the blimp descends, beginning aerial mastication and pre-digestion as chunks are passed back

**Feeding:**
- The blimp lowers feeding tentacles from beneath its envelope to collect large chunks
- Exovants continue dismemberment work and serve as perimeter guards during the feeding process
- All metabolic waste and excess heat is purged after reintegration through the main carrier body

**Recovery:**
After feeding, all viable exovants reattach to the blimp and resume metabolic cycling. Exovant loss is rare and a significant event in the lifecycle of the organism. Recovery from damage is possible through womb partifunct regeneration over the course of days.

---

## Exovant Characterisation

**Definition (Updated):**
An exovant is a persistent, non-autonomous, multi-partifunct hunting module that depends on a host composite organism (the blimp) for long-term metabolic needs. Exovants are reusable and reintegrate fully after each hunt.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Duration Unplugged | 2–4 hours |
| Lifecycle | Long-lived unless destroyed in action |
| Integration | Socket-plug vascular + hormonal channels |
| Coordination | Highly social, chemically synchronised |

**Anatomy:**
- 4 locomotive limbs
- 2 manipulator limbs
- Front-mounted jaw partifunct similar to terrestrial canid structures
- Hardened cranial ridge for gliding impacts

---

## Morphology

**Main Blimp Body:**
- Length: 6–8 metres
- Gas Volume: Multi-chambered lift sacs with buoyancy control
- Feeding System: Extendable tentacles with food-receiving ports
- Womb Partifunct: Internal structure used for slow exovant regeneration

**Exovants:**
- Length: ~1.2 metres
- Configuration: Hexapedal with central cognitive and circulatory node
- Communication: Acoustic pulse clicks + chemical trail tags
- Function: Kill, guard, butcher, transfer

---

## Ecological Role

- Apex Predator in multiple steppe regions
- Controls grazer population levels across diverse taxa
- Engages in nutrient redistribution via partial carrion left after feeding
- High learning capacity — shows avoidance behaviour after losing exovants to injury or organised prey defence
