# B013 – Structural and Behavioural Comparison of Womb and Non-Womb Reproducers

## Summary

This dispatch outlines and contrasts the two dominant reproductive architectures on TPIV: Womb Assembly Reproduction and Non-Womb Environmental Assembly.

---

## I. Womb Reproduction – Internal Assembly Model

**Mechanism:**
- Composite construction occurs within a specialised womb partifunct
- Parent modules generate partifzygotes via environmental GFP (eGFP) recombination
- Partifzygotes are polled and integrated within the womb for full composite assembly

**Key Features:**
- Epigenetic transfer stabilises generational form
- Birth occurs with all modules in metabolic and structural unity
- Supports high PSV (60–90%) and moderate MDI (2–6), depending on ecological niche

**Examples:**

| Taxon | PSV | MDI |
|-------|-----|-----|
| Zebra Grazer | 83.2 | 4.6 |
| Bronze Crest Diver | 74.4 | 3.1 |
| Drophunter Blimp | 67.8 | 5.5 |

---

## II. Non-Womb Reproduction – Environmental Assembly

**Mechanism:**
- Reproduction occurs without a womb organ, via autonomous assembly of viable GFP+BLU units in the environment
- Partifzygotes form spontaneously in the presence of compatible ecological conditions (moisture, salinity, chemical gradients)

**Key Features:**
- Mass dispersal leads to ecological flooding of genetic configurations
- Little-to-no epigenetic continuity — form determined by environmental pressure
- Supports low PSV (5–30%) and high MDI (6–10+)

**Examples:**

| Taxon | PSV | MDI |
|-------|-----|-----|
| Clam Grasses | 24.1 | 7.2 |
| Stalk Trees | 16.3 | 8.9 |
| Flying Grass | 11.4 | 9.5 |

---

## III. Comparative Table

| Trait | Womb Reproduction | Non-Womb Reproduction |
|-------|------------------|-----------------------|
| Assembly Site | Internal (womb organ) | External (environmental) |
| Partifzygote Coordination | Centralised & polled | Distributed & opportunistic |
| Juvenile Coherence | High | Low |
| PSV Range | ~60–90% | ~5–30% |
| MDI Range | ~2–6 | ~6–10+ |
| Generational Continuity | Moderate to strong | Weak to stochastic |
| Reproductive Cost | High (per individual) | Low (per unit) |
| Ecological Reach | Focused/stable habitats | Broad/dispersal-driven |

---

## IV. Ecological Implications

- High-stress, niche-stable environments (e.g., steppe) favour high-PSV, low-MDI womb reproducers
- Volatile, resource-fluctuating zones (e.g., intertidal basins, flying forest) favour low-PSV, high-MDI non-womb reproducers

---

## Conclusion

The bifurcation between womb and non-womb lineages represents a deep divergence in evolutionary strategy on TPIV. PSV and MDI offer a unified language for taxon modelling across this divide, allowing quantitative modelling of stability, adaptability, and convergence — across composite systems where even the definition of "individual" is fluid.
