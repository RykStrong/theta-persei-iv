# B015 – Separated Behaviors and Exovant Structures

## Summary

This dispatch defines **Separated Behaviors (SBs)** — short-term, function-specific detachment of specialised modular units from host composite organisms. These detaching units, called **Exovants**, carry out tasks including hunting, manipulation, or sensory exploration, returning to the host for reintegration.

---

## I. Formal Definition

**Separated Behavior (SB):**
> The deployment of a temporarily autonomous biological structure (Exovant) from a composite organism for the purpose of executing a discrete functional behaviour before reintegration or expiration.

**Exovant *(n.)*:**
> A complex, metabolically bounded structure derived from the parent composite, typically incapable of long-term survival or reproduction.

---

## II. Documented SB Taxa

### 1. Throwjaw (TP-THJ-3A)
- **Function:** High-speed predatory strike
- **Structure:** Jaw + cranial stalk segment
- **Reintegration:** Typically via retrieval and reattachment
- **Notable Feature:** Blood and nerve socket resealing during reattachment

### 2. Tossclaw (TP-TSC-2M)
- **Function:** Manipulation, grasping, environmental clearance
- **Deployment:** Tendon-driven ejection from shoulder girdle
- **Recovery:** Manual reabsorption or abandonment

### 3. Drophunter (TP-DRP-7B)
- **Function:** Coordinated ambush predation
- **Deployment:** 6–12 dog-like Exovants launched from aerial blimp body
- **Duration:** Up to 4 hours independent
- **Reintegration:** Re-docking for metabolic processing
- **Notable:** Only known Exovants with full limb articulation and mastication capability

### 4. Marine Drophunter (TP-MDR-7F)
- **Adaptation:** Water-capable Exovants with gas inflation sacs for prey flotation
- **Recovery:** Via tentacle-assisted blimp landing or buoyancy lift
- **Nesting:** Blimps raise offspring in Flying Forest canopies

---

## III. Structural and Functional Notes

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Energy Limitation | Exovants operate on stored glucose reserves; must reintegrate or perish |
| Waste Constraints | No elimination mechanisms; limited metabolic envelope |
| Sensory/Motor Independence | All observed Exovants retain specialised sensory and motor function, sometimes including memory buffers |
| Attachment Sites | Vascular + neural ports standard on host composites for rapid docking |
