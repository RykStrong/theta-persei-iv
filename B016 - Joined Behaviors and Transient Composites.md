# B016 – Joined Behaviors and Transient Composites

## Summary

This dispatch addresses **Joined Behaviors** — the temporary aggregation of multiple independently viable organisms into a single transient composite. These Transient Modular Composites are not exovant-based, but rely on shared signalling, mechanical coupling, or ecological synchrony to form larger functioning units for flight, foraging, or display.

---

## I. Definitions

**Joined Behavior (JB):**
> The temporary and cooperative physical or behavioural union of two or more composite organisms into a larger coordinated structure.

**Transient Modular Composite (TMC):**
> A biomechanical configuration composed of two or more full organisms, each retaining reproductive and metabolic independence, functioning jointly for enhanced task performance.

---

## II. Documented JB Taxa

### 1. Stringbird Composite (TP-STB-C4T)
- **Function:** Aerodynamic glider-formation for long-range travel
- **Mechanism:** Four individual avians dock using temporary glandular adhesion pads
- **Coordination:** Real-time signalling via chemical pulses and low-frequency sound
- **Duration:** 12–18 hours
- **Role:** Migration, cross-valley navigation, predator evasion

### 2. Ribbonfish Chain (TP-RBN-5L)
- **Function:** Aquatic meandering for energy-efficient foraging
- **Structure:** Interlocking dorsal and ventral fins form a continuous wave conduit
- **Communication:** Lateral line oscillation + iridescent flash-pulses
- **Duration:** Variable; disperses upon successful feeding or light-shift

---

## III. Structural and Behavioural Notes

- No permanent tissue fusion
- BLU systems remain isolated within each organism
- Intercommunication via passive synchronisation or DO-mite exchange (pending confirmation)
- Frequent dissolution and reformation with flexible membership

---

## IV. Classification Status

- **Not DOs** — each node remains independently viable
- **Not SBs** — no structure detachment or metabolic isolation
- Distinct from colonies or swarms — temporary, strategic groupings with composite biomechanical effect
