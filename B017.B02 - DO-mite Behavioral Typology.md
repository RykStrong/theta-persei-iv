# B017.B02 – DO-mite Behavioural Typology and Interactions

## Summary

This dispatch provides a classification of observed DO-mite behaviours and inter-node interactions within Distributed Organisms (DOs). These insect-like CEP carriers not only transmit biochemical cognition exchange packets but also play a key role in maintaining the coordinated functionality of DOs across multiple environments. The DO-mites and their hosting 'organs' are a set of partifuncts.

---

## Behaviour Classes of DO-mites

### 1. Relay Flight
The default behaviour of DO-mites:
- Travel between partivants using tight looping paths
- Dock at CEP ports to retrieve and deliver CEPs
- Return to central partivant for base synchronisation (if applicable)

**Observed Patterns:**
- 4–7 second intervals per node in high-density units (e.g., Horse DO-Grazer)
- 15–25 second round-trip lag in loosely grouped partivants (e.g., DO Pack Hunters)

### 2. Redundancy Sweep
A fail-safe behaviour used to verify delivery:
- DO-mites duplicate CEPs with unique decay tags
- Multiple mites deliver the same CEP to different nodes
- Redundant CEPs allow convergence on a synchronised state after interruptions

### 3. Interference and Defense
Airspace contestation between rival DOs is now confirmed:
- DO-mites exhibit swarming and jamming behaviours when encountering foreign CEP carriers
- May attempt to disrupt rival CEP transmission via mid-air collisions or biochemical release (pheromone fogging)
- Physical attrition of mites in these conflicts is significant, yet tolerated

### 4. Mating Flight / Recombination Behaviour
While not confirmed, several instances of:
- DO-mite pair-contact in neutral airspace
- Swapping of CEP fragments
- Return with altered behavioural state or routing priorities

These behaviours may suggest intraspecific learning, epigenetic adaptation, or GFP-based pherotype updating.

---

## Interaction Zones & Density

### A. Swarm Envelopes
Most partivant nodes are surrounded by 5–20 DO-mites at all times:
- Tight clouds within 3–5 metres
- Constant rotation and CEP exchange
- More dense in central or higher-order control nodes

### B. Transit Corridors
DO-mites tend to follow established flyways between partivants:
- Ground-level: Skimming over terrain to minimise wind disruption
- Elevated DOs: Corridor defined by signal gradients and thermal drafts
- Transit paths may become visible during high-density traffic, resembling shimmering filaments

### C. Airspace Conflict Zones
Identified in mixed-biome contact zones (e.g., forest/steppe borders):
- Overlapping swarm envelopes lead to high CEP corruption rates
- DOs appear to adopt active avoidance of DOs with incompatible CEP types

## Communication Rates and CEP Complexity

CEPs carry 3–5 tiers of data:
1. Node Status
2. Local Environment Reading
3. Behavioural Directive
4. *(Optional)* Inter-node Priority
5. *(Optional)* Genetic Broadcast or Update Seed

CEPs are timestamped chemically. DO-mites ignore packets below freshness threshold, leading to the development of "soft real-time cognition" in DO behaviour.
