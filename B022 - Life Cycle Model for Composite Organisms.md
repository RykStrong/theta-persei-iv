# B022 – Life Cycle Model for Composite Organisms

*Topic: Assembly-Conception and the Role of Partifzygotes*

## Overview

The composite lifeforms of TPIV reproduce via a modular, multi-stage process that bypasses classical fertilisation. The process hinges on the formation of **partifzygotes** — recombined GFP bundles specific to each partifunct — followed by their assembly into a coherent organism within a womb partifunct.

---

## Stage 1 – GFP Generation & Environmental Exchange

- Each GFP particle originates from a mature partifunct's fruiting body (microbial, insect-like, plant-like)
- GFP serves dual roles:
  - **Nutrient** — Most are consumed and recycled
  - **Reproductive Vector** — A select subset enters gestation pathways
- Environmental GFP (eGFP) saturates TPIV's biosphere and is gathered passively (via ingestion, stomatal uptake, or specialised GFP-trap organs)
- Each resident partifunct continually:
  - Seeks out copies of its own GFP in the eGFP pool
  - Performs recombination with eGFP or peer GFP to produce a **partifzygote**

---

## Stage 2 – Assembly-Conception Initiation

- Under hormonal triggers, the womb partifunct becomes receptive
- Each resident partifunct transfers its partifzygote into the womb
- The womb:
  - Polls the surrounding partifuncts for expression guidance
  - Issues epigenetic control signals (chemical) to influence BLU expression — body layout, symmetry, sensory placement
- Substitution or deletion of partifzygotes is possible based on environmental feedback or internal quorum sensing
- Resulting structure: a composite embryo formed of all partifzygotes + a coordinating, enveloping layer of neonatal BLU tissue

---

## Stage 3 – Neonatal Development & Womb Expression

- The embryo attaches to the womb wall
- Each partifunct begins expressing:
  - Limb Partifuncts → legs, wings, or fins
  - Digestive Partifuncts → internal tract networks
  - Sensory Partifuncts → clustered arrays of lenses, fronds, or pressure domes
- Partifuncts remain metabolically autonomous but form:
  - Shared vascular interfaces
  - Coordinated hormonal loops
- Development is flexible and individualised, leading to major anatomical diversity between siblings

---

## Stage 4 – Birth and Independent Metabolic Life

- The organism is born once viable
- Typical birth variations:
  - Hexapod base form (common but not universal)
  - Multiple circulatory and respiratory systems
  - Respiratory stomata and distributed micro-lung partifuncts
- Newborns are distinct from parents; family resemblance is weak
- Embodied partifuncts begin shedding GFP to rejoin the eGFP stream immediately after birth

---

## Stage 5 – Adult Metabolism and Genetic Acquisition

- Adult grazers consume plant-phase partifuncts, some of which are genetic kin
- eGFP also enters through:
  - Tracheal exchange
  - Dermal absorption
  - Internal symbiont processing
- Each partifunct:
  - Filters the eGFP stream
  - Selects compatible strands of its own GFP
  - Begins recombination again, preparing future partifzygotes
- Ageing may alter the expression of some partifuncts, resulting in new appendages or metabolic pathways

---

## Stage 6 – Next-Generation Assembly-Conception

- When conditions align, partifzygotes are routed to the womb partifunct again
- eGFP continues to supplement the local genetic pool
- A new assembly occurs, repeating the modular reproductive cycle

---

## Key Takeaways

- Partifzygotes are the foundational reproductive units on TPIV — modular, recombined, and transient
- There is no true fertilisation or parental genome unification
- The womb partifunct is a specialised, information-gathering and instructive interface, not simply a container
- Partifuncts balance conservatism (replicating proven forms) with innovation (lottery-style recombination)
- The embryonic organism is assembled, not grown from a zygote in the Earth sense

## Addendum B022a – Non-Womb Assembly-Conception in Autotrophic and Low-Order Forms

*Authors: Dr. Arjun Patel, Dr. Maya Chen | Field Review: Dr. Reiko M'bara | Timestamp: TPIV Local Day 70*

While most higher composite organisms rely on a womb partifunct to coordinate the assembly of partifzygotes into structured embryos, a significant class of lifeforms — primarily autotrophs and basal organisms — engage in **non-womb assembly-conception**. This process occurs when individual eGFP particles accumulate enough wrapping BLU tissue to self-organise and metabolise spontaneously, without gestation inside a host body.

### Mechanism
- Some GFP bundles are shed with partial or complete BLU envelopes already expressed
- These GFP+BLU units may fuse with other such units, or initiate self-metabolism if sufficient structural integrity is present
- Fusion occurs when hormonal gradients or environmental triggers (heat, chemical cues, moonlight phases) are met and recombination compatibility is within viable bounds

### Ecological Examples
- Autotrophic mats ("clamshell grass," "obsidian mats") often form via slow soil-level fusion of independently metabolising units
- Floating phototrophs over the central inland sea can merge in situ to form functional colonies
- Spore-like photosynthetic droplets suspended in air over canopies have been observed forming tiny viable composites after clustering

### Evolutionary Implication
- Non-womb assembly is likely an ancestral mechanism predating the evolution of womb partifuncts
- This reproductive mode favours rapid ecological colonisation, wider genetic diffusion, and faster adaptation cycles
- It also supports the distributed reproduction strategies seen in many rootlike and floating taxa
