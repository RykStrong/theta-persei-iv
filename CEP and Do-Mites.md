# CEP and Do-Mites

**CEP Definition:** Chemical, Electrical, and Pheromonal signaling systems  

**[DO-Mites](DO-Mites.md):** Tiny airborne partivants that carry hormonal and neurological information  

**Signature Feature:** Cloud of do-mites flying in/out of partivant openings  

**Function:** Enable coordination across physically separated bodies

---

## CEP Communication System

### Four Signal Vectors

**Chemical:**
- Hormones dissolved in fluids
- Metabolic signals
- Nutrient status indicators
- Growth regulation

**Electrical:**
- Some lineages use bioelectric signals
- Neural-like pulses
- Rapid coordination
- Short-range (touching partivants)

**Pheromonal:**
- Airborne chemical signals
- Territory marking
- Alarm signals
- Reproductive status
- Long-range communication

GFP
- GFP is used to 'sign' the DO-Mites for security 
- use of GFP to transfer neurological 'state' information


**Integration:**
[Partivants](Partivant%20Definition.md) communicate mainly using tiny airborne (insect-sized) partivants called **do-mites**, which carry hormonal and neurological information vectors.   The information can be rich enough to eneable shared cognition.

---

## Do-Mite Functions

### Information Carriers

**Hormonal Messages:**
- Carry hormones between partivants
- Metabolic coordination signals
- Growth regulation chemicals
- Stress indicators

**Neurological Information:**
- Neural-analog signals encoded chemically and with GFP
- The rapid rate of Do-Mite communication enables cognitive process sharing
- Memory transfer (simple patterns)
- Behavioral coordination
- Sensory data transmission

**Nutrient Transport:**
- Small amounts of processed nutrients
- Blood sugar analogs
- Critical metabolites
- Emergency energy delivery

**Status Updates:**
- Partivant health signals
- Resource availability
- Threat detection
- Reproductive readiness

## Do-Mite Behavior

### Flight Patterns

**[Behavioral Modes](DO-Mites.md):**
- **Outbound:** Carry message from source partivant
- **Inbound:** Deliver message to target partivant
- **Patrol:** Monitor area between partivants
- **Swarm:** Coordinated mass movement

**Navigation:**
- Chemical gradients (find home partivants)
- Visual landmarks (in some species)
- [Radiation sensing](Radita.md) (long-range navigation)
- Pheromone trails (follow established routes)

**Entry/Exit Points:**
- Specialized spiracles on partivants
- Do-mites enter carrying external info
- Exit carrying partivant's response
- Continuous traffic (thousands per hour in large DOs)

## Swarm Organization

### [Envelopes and Corridors](CEP%20and%20Do-Mites.md)

**Swarm Envelope:**
- Dense cloud surrounding partivant
- Immediate communication range
- Defensive barrier (visual obscuration)
- Hundreds to thousands of do-mites

**Corridors:**
- Established flight paths between partivants
- Higher do-mite density
- Follow terrain features
- Maintained by pheromone trails

**Field Effect:**
- Multiple partivants create overlapping envelopes
- Corridors connect envelopes
- Creates communication network topology
- Visible from distance (shimmering clouds)

## Communication Range

### Distance Limitations

**Close Range (0-100m):**
- Direct do-mite flight
- High bandwidth (many mites)
- Rapid response (seconds to minutes)
- Most DO partivants operate in this range

**Medium Range (100m-1km):**
- Relay via intermediate swarms
- Lower bandwidth
- Delayed response (minutes to hours)
- Pheromone trails important

**Long Range (>1km):**
- Pheromone trails primary
- Very low bandwidth
- Slow response (hours to days)

**Maximum Observed:**
- Chemical DOs: ~5km (extreme cases)
- Most stay within 1km

## Do-Mite Production

### Where They Come From

**Specialized Partivants:**
- Some partivants dedicated to do-mite production
- "Hive partivants" or "Courier factories"
- Continuous mite generation
- High metabolic cost

**Distributed Production:**
- Most partivants produce some do-mites
  using a dedicated partifunct
- Low-level constant production
- Ramps up during high communication needs
- Energy trade-off
- Easier to manage 

**Lifespan:**
- Individual do-mite: Hours to days
- Continuous replacement needed
- Old mites recycled (eaten by partivants)
- Metabolically expensive strategy

---

## Chemical Cargo

### What Do-Mites Carry

**Hormone Types:**
- Growth signals (partivant coordination)
- Stress hormones (threat response)
- Metabolic regulators (energy distribution)
- Reproductive signals (mating coordination)

**Encoding:**
- Chemical concentration = signal strength
- Chemical combinations = complex messages
- Time-release chemicals (sustained signals)
- Volatile vs. persistent compounds

**Capacity:**
- Limited by do-mite size
- ~0.1-1 microliter cargo volume
- Multiple trips for complex messages
- Redundancy via many mites

## Vulnerabilities

### Communication Disruption

**[Predation on Swarms](DO-Mite%20conflict.md):**
- Some predators target do-mite clouds
- Disrupts DO coordination
- Partivants become isolated
- Critical vulnerability

**Environmental:**
- High winds scatter swarms
- Rain grounds do-mites
- Cold slows flight
- Dense vegetation blocks corridors

**Intentional Interference:**
- Other DOs may disrupt swarms
- Chemical jamming (pheromone confusion)
- Physical barriers (territorial defense)
- [Airspace conflicts](DO-Mite%20conflict.md) common

## Observational Field Notes

### Recognizing DOs

**Visual Signature:**
*"Cloud of 'insects' is actually the nervouse and hormone systems of the creature"* — Field Note, Day 32

**Close Observation:**
- Swarms shimmer in sunlight
- Constant traffic in/out of partivant spiracles
- Coordinated movement across landscape
- Different from actual insects (too organized)

**Sound:**
- Humming/buzzing from wing beats
- Louder near partivants (high density)
- Changes pitch with swarm activity level
- Distinctive from insect swarms
