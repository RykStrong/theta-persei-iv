# Circulatory Systems

**Architecture:** Multiple independent circulatory loops, not single unified system  
**Key Feature:** Each partifunct has own circulation, connected via transfer organs  
**Hearts:** Many small distributed hearts, not one large heart  
**Result:** Resistant to circulatory collapse, hard to bleed to death

---

## Distributed Architecture

### Multiple Independent Systems

**Not Unified Circulation:**
There is no true homogeneity of gross anatomy among life-forms. Each partifunct's organs have their own blood circulation. Some organisms have a partifunct dedicated to blood/circulation, others do not.

**Typical Configuration:**
- Each major [[Partifunct Core Definition|partifunct]] maintains own circulatory loop
- Limb partifuncts have local circulation
- Digestive system has own blood supply
- Brain partifuncts heavily vascularized
- Respiratory partifuncts connect to all systems

**Connection Points:**
- [[34c-Transfer-Organs|Transfer organs]] link different circulatory systems
- Capillary exchange at partifunct boundaries
- Shared organs where capillaries infiltrate to within single membrane
- Hormonal and nutrient exchange

---

## Multiple Hearts

### Distributed Pumping

**Many Small vs One Large:**
Frequently, blood is pumped using many small heart partifuncts and oxygenated via many small lung partifuncts.

**Advantages:**
- Redundancy: Loss of one heart not fatal
- Local control: Each region can adjust flow independently
- Resistant to circulatory collapse
- Harder to bleed to death
- Distributed energy cost

**Locations:**
- [[34b-Hearts-and-Reservoirs|Hearts scattered throughout body]]
- Near major muscle groups
- Along central body cavity
- Some at limb bases
- Variable number (4-20+ depending on organism size)

## Blood and Fluid Types

### Oxygen Transport

**Oxygen-Carrying Fluids:**
- Protein-based oxygen carriers (not hemoglobin necessarily)
- Color varies by lineage (red, blue, green observed)
- 25% O₂ atmosphere supports efficient transport
- High oxygen capacity needed for active metabolism

**Multiple Fluid Types:**
- Some organisms have separate systems for:
  - Oxygen transport
  - Nutrient transport
  - Waste removal
- Others combine functions in single fluid

---

## Transfer Organs

### Inter-Partifunct Exchange

**Critical Structures:**
Shared organs, where the capillaries of two partifuncts infiltrate to within a single membrane of each other, are used to:
- Pass blood sugar
- Clean blood (waste removal)
- Spread hormones

**Function:**
- [[34c-Transfer-Organs|Capillary exchange organs]] at boundaries
- No direct mixing of blood from different partifuncts
- Diffusion across membrane barriers
- Maintains genetic independence while enabling metabolic cooperation

**Locations:**
- Limb-to-girdle interfaces
- Digestive-to-body connections
- Brain-to-body supply routes
- Respiratory exchange points

---

## Vascular Architecture

### Within Partifuncts

**Local Networks:**
- Arteries (high pressure, away from heart)
- Veins (low pressure, return to heart)
- Capillary beds (exchange sites)
- Often more complex branching than Earth organisms

**[[LU-Biology|BLU-Scale Circulation]]:**
- Vessels between large BLU structures
- Cytoplasmic streaming within BLU tissue
- Nutrient delivery to [[LU-Biology|genode clusters]]
- Waste removal pathways

---

## Pressure and Flow

### Regulation Mechanisms

**Multiple Control Points:**
- Each heart responds to local demands
- Chemical signals coordinate activity
- [[33-Brain-Partifuncts|Brain partifuncts]] provide central coordination
- Stress hormones increase overall circulation

**Pressure Variation:**
- Higher pressure in active regions (muscles, brain)
- Lower pressure in storage regions (digestive reserves)
- Variable pressure across different partifunct systems
- Transfer organs handle pressure differentials

---

## Resistance to Shock

### Hard to Kill

**Composite Organisms Can Be Hard to Kill:**
- Resistant to circulatory collapse
- Harder to bleed to death

**Mechanisms:**
- Multiple hearts provide backup
- Local circulation continues even if central systems damaged
- Transfer organs can close/isolate damaged regions
- Distributed reserves maintain blood volume
- Rapid clotting at wound sites

**Limitations:**
- Massive trauma can still overwhelm system
- Critical partifuncts (brain, certain organs) still vulnerable
- Coordinated function eventually fails
- [[Death-and-Decomposition|Death appears Earth-familiar once it occurs]]

---

## Metabolic Support

### Energy Distribution

**Nutrient Delivery:**
- Digestive system absorbs nutrients
- Transfer organs distribute to other partifuncts
- Each region receives appropriate fuel
- Energy-intensive tissues (brain, muscle) get priority

**Waste Removal:**
- Metabolic byproducts collected
- Transferred to excretory organs
- Some partifuncts specialized for waste processing
- Toxic buildup prevented by multiple pathways

---

## Oxygen Distribution

### Coordination with Respiratory Systems

**[[Respiratory-Systems|Lung Interface]]:**
- Multiple small lungs oxygenate blood
- Each lung may supply local region primarily
- Transfer organs distribute oxygenated blood
- Deoxygenated blood returns via venous systems

**High Oxygen Efficiency:**
- 25% O₂ atmosphere enables efficient uptake
- Supports high metabolic rates
- Allows large active organisms
- Enables [[33-Brain-Partifuncts|complex cognition]]

---

## Variable Architectures

### Organism-Specific Patterns

**Simple Organisms:**
- Few partifuncts = few circulatory loops
- May lack dedicated heart partifuncts
- Simple diffusion sufficient for small bodies
- Minimal transfer organ complexity

**Complex Organisms:**
- Many partifuncts = complex vascular network
- Multiple specialized heart types
- Elaborate transfer organ systems
- Coordinated central regulation

**[[50-Distributed-Organisms-Overview|Distributed Organisms]]:**
- Each partivant has own complete circulation
- No direct vascular connection between bodies
- [[51-CEP-and-Do-Mites|DO-mites]] may carry nutrients/signals
- Complete independence between separated bodies

## Research Observations

### Comparative Studies

**Across Taxa:**
- [[TPM-Zebra-Grazer|Zebra Grazers]]: 8-12 hearts typical
- Large predators: 15-20 hearts
- Small organisms: 2-4 hearts
- [[45-Flying-Forest|Aerial organisms]]: Reduced mass, efficient pumping

**Trauma Response:**
- Damage to single heart: Organism continues functioning
- Loss of multiple hearts: Reduced activity but survival possible
- Catastrophic damage: Eventually fatal but delayed
- Remarkable resilience compared to Earth organisms
