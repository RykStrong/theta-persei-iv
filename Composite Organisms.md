# Composite Organisms

**Definition:** Animals made up of multiple genetically distinct [partifuncts](Partifunct%20Core%20Definition.md). Partifuncts integrate into Composite Organisms, where each performs specific biological functions analogous to organs or zooids on Earth.

**Key Feature:** Appears as single individual, but is coalition of genetic lineages  
**Integration:** Tight physical, metabolic, and behavioral coordination  

---

## What is a Composite Organism?

All of the 'plants' and 'animals' on TPIV are Composite Organisms (CO).
No single genome defines the CO.

### Coalition, Not Individual

**Genetic Reality:**
- 10-50+ distinct genetic lineages in one body
- Each partifunct has unique [GFP](GFP%20and%20Genodules.md)
- No shared genome between parts
- No single genome defines the whole
- Each part descended from different ancestral organism

**Functional Reality:**
- Operates as single integrated unit
- Coordinated behavior and physiology
- Unified response to environment
- Appears as individual to observer

**Paradox:**
- Genetically: Multiple organisms
- Functionally: Single organism
- **Answer:** Both perspectives valid, context-dependent
