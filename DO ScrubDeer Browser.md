# DO-ScrubDeer Browser

**Classification:** Grazer-Mobile—Large Individuals (Distributed)  
**Biome:** [Apanon](Continents.md) scrublands and grasslands  
**PSV:** 76% (±1σ)  
**MDI:** 5.4  
**Communication:** Chemical [do-mites](CEP%20and%20Do-Mites.md)

---

## Overview

### Distributed Herbivore

**DO-ScrubDeer Browser** is one of the most studied [Distributed Organisms](Distributed%20Organisms%20Overview.md), representing a successful herbivore strategy on [Apanon](Continents.md).

**Key Features:**
- Multiple [partivant](Partivant%20Definition.md) types (feeding, digestive, sensory)
- [Do-mite](CEP%20and%20Do-Mites.md) cloud communication
- Chameleon-like skin for visual signaling
- Moderately high PSV, elevated MDI (adaptive variation)

---

## Partivant Types

### Specialized Bodies

**Feeding Nodes:**
Small, agile, quadrupedal bodies about half the size of Earth-deer, each specialized in cropping grass and low vegetation.

**Structure:**
- Four legs (sometimes six in variants)
- Simple mouth with grinding plates
- Minimal digestive tract
- Enhanced mobility

**Function:**
- Each node ingests vegetation
- Processes it minimally
- Internally forms nutrient-rich pellets encapsulated with [GFP](GFP%20and%20Genodules.md) packets
- Passes pellets to digestor partivants

**Distinctive Feature:**
Have distinctively patterned, chameleon-like skins used for signaling and predator confusion.

---

### Digestive Nodes ("Fermenters")

Large, sluggish units that remain stationary or move slowly; have massive internal chambers for microbial fermentation.

**Structure:**
- Large body (2-3× feeding node size)
- Minimal legs (reduced mobility)
- Huge internal fermentation chambers
- Wide, receptive mouth

**Function:**
- Grazers periodically approach fermenters
- Deposit nutrient pellets directly into fermenter mouths
- Fermentation breaks down cellulose (symbiotic microbes)
- After digestion, distribute nutrient-rich solutions back to feeding nodes

**Distribution System:**
Via specialized [mite-like carriers](CEP%20and%20Do-Mites.md) (do-mites modified for nutrient transport).

---

### Sensory Nodes

**Structure:**
- Long necks (elevated observation)
- Loud voices (alarm calls)
- Enhanced vision ([visual partifuncts](Sensory%20Systems.md))
- Acute hearing

**Function:**
- Watch for predators
- Monitor herd movements
- Coordinate feeding node positions
- Alert system for entire DO

**Behavior:**
- Position themselves at herd periphery
- Elevated heads scan horizon
- Constant [do-mite](CEP%20and%20Do-Mites.md) exchange with other partivants
- Vocal warnings trigger coordinated response

---

### Defensive Nodes

**Structure (when present):**
- Heavier build
- Thicker skin/armor
- Horns or other defensive structures
- Enhanced muscle mass

**Function:**
- Protect feeding and digestive nodes
- Confront predators
- Created as needed (environmental response)

**Note:**
Not always present—[womb](Womb%20Partifunct%20Systems.md) creates defensive partivants only in high-predation environments.

---

### Cognitive Nodes

**Structure:**
- Enhanced [brain partifuncts](Nervous%20Systems.md)
- Multiple cognitive modules (MEMO, STRA, NAVI)
- Well-protected central body position
- Moderate size

**Function:**
- [Distributed cognition](Distributed%20Organisms%20Overview.md) coordination
- Memory storage (migration routes, danger areas)
- Strategic planning (where to graze, when to move)
- Decision-making integration

**Distribution:**
Typically 1-2 cognitive partivants per DO, positioned centrally among feeding nodes.

## Communication System

### Do-Mite Swarms

**Observational Note (Day 32):**
*"Cloud of 'insects' is actually the communication and circulatory systems of the creature"*

**Swarm Characteristics:**
- Thousands of [do-mites](CEP%20and%20Do-Mites.md) per DO
- Constant traffic between partivants
- [Corridors](CEP%20and%20Do-Mites.md) connect feeding nodes to fermenters
- Dense envelope around sensory nodes (high information flow)

**Function:**
- Hormonal coordination (when to feed, when to flee)
- Neurological signals (threat detection, movement coordination)
- Nutrient distribution (processed food from fermenters)
- Status updates (partivant health, resource needs)

---

## Visual Signaling

### Chameleon-Like Skin

**Capability:**
Feeding nodes have distinctively patterned, chameleon-like skins used for:
- **Signaling:** Coordinate with other partivants visually
- **Predator confusion:** Disruptive coloration, pattern changes
- **Emotional state:** Stress patterns, calm patterns
- **Identity:** Individual recognition within DO

**Patterns:**
- Resting: Muted browns, greens (camouflage)
- Alarmed: High-contrast stripes, spots
- Feeding: Coordinated patterns (synchronization signal)
- Mating season: Bright colors (reproductive partivants)

---

## Feeding Behavior

### Coordinated Grazing

**Process:**
1. Feeding nodes spread across grazing area
2. [Do-mites](CEP%20and%20Do-Mites.md) maintain coordination
3. Sensory nodes monitor for threats
4. Each feeding node crops vegetation
5. Minimal internal processing (forms pellet)
6. Periodically approach fermenter partivants
7. Deposit pellets in fermenter mouths
8. Receive nutrient solution via do-mites
9. Return to grazing

**Efficiency:**
- Division of labor (feeding vs. processing)
- Fermenters stationary (energy conservation)
- Feeding nodes mobile (resource exploitation)
- Continuous operation (some nodes always feeding)

## Population Structure

### Partivant Distribution

**Typical DO Composition:**
- 8-15 feeding partivants
- 2-4 digestive partivants (fermenters)
- 2-3 sensory partivants
- 0-2 defensive partivants (optional)
- 1-2 cognitive partivants
- 0-1 reproductive partivants (seasonal)

**Total:** 13-27 partivants per organism

**Variation:**
High [MDI](Taxa%20and%20Metrics.md) (5.4) indicates significant variation in partivant number and types across individuals.

---

## Reproduction

### Seasonal Reproductive Partivants

**Created as needed for lekking or other genetic exchange needs.**

**Process:**
- One fermenter partivant develops [womb partifunct](Womb%20Partifunct%20Systems.md)
- Or dedicated reproductive partivant assembled
- Collects [eGFP](Environmental%20GFP.md) from environment (grazing areas)
- Assembles multiple offspring partivants
- Birth produces complete DO (all partivant types)
- [Do-mite](CEP%20and%20Do-Mites.md) communication functional immediately

**Challenge:**
High failure rate—coordinating multiple partivants from birth is complex.

## Ecological Role

### Keystone Herbivore

**Impact:**
- Primary grazer in Apanon scrublands
- Controls vegetation growth
- [Cultivates](Behavior.md) preferred grasses (inadvertent farming)
- Prey for [rDO-HillWolf](DO%20HillWolf%20PackHunter.md) and other predators

**Behavior:**
- Nomadic grazing patterns
- Follow seasonal grass growth
- [NAVI brain](Nervous%20Systems.md) in cognitive partivants stores migration routes
- Territory marking via pheromones

---

## Predator Interactions

### Defense Strategies

**Early Warning:**
- Sensory partivants detect threats at distance
- Alarm calls trigger coordinated response
- [Do-mites](CEP%20and%20Do-Mites.md) carry panic signals

**Evasion:**
- Feeding nodes scatter (confuse predator)
- Chameleon skin flashes (disorientation)
- Fermenters remain stationary (too large to flee)
- Cognitive partivants direct escape routes

**Resilience:**
- Loss of 1-2 feeding nodes not critical
- DO continues functioning
- Can regenerate lost partivants over time
- Fermenter loss more serious (metabolic disruption)

## Comparative Notes

### River-Crossing Variant

*"The population sampled near the Olan River crossing exhibited a PSV of 92% (indicating high partifunct consistency) but had an elevated MDI of 5.4. The river-crossing behavior appears to encourage wider morphological expression—approximately 35% more variable than average populations, likely reflecting adaptive pressures."*

**Adaptations:**
- Some feeding partivants more aquatic
- Enhanced swimming capability
- Fermenters positioned on riverbanks
- [Do-mite corridors](CEP%20and%20Do-Mites.md) cross water (limited range)

## Research Observations

### Field Studies

**Population Density:**
- 3-8 DOs per square kilometer (typical)
- Higher in rich grasslands
- Lower in scrub/marginal habitats

**Partivant Spacing:**
- Feeding nodes: 10-50m from fermenter
- Sensory nodes: Peripheral (100-200m range)
- Cognitive nodes: Central, near fermenters
- Maximum DO diameter: ~500m typical

**Do-Mite Traffic:**
- Peak during feeding (high coordination)
- Lower at night (reduced activity)
- Spikes during threat response
- Continuous low-level background
