Distributed Organisms (DO) on Apanon represent composite life forms where essential biological functions are distributed across physically separated bodies (partivants). Extended field study reveals that cognitive integration depends entirely on DO-mite carriers—microscopic partivants that shuttle hormonal and neurological information between larger partivants.

## Do-Mites

### Living Communication Packets

**What Are Do-Mites:**
- Tiny airborne [[Partivant-Definition|partivants]] themselves
- Insect-sized (2-15mm typically)
- Specialized for carrying information
- Not viable independent (like all partivants)

**Structure:**
- Wings or gas bladders (flight)
- Chemical storage organs (hormone cargo)
- Minimal digestive system
- Simple [[33-Brain-Partifuncts|nervous system]] (navigation only)
- Sensory organs (find target partivants)

**Signature Feature:**
A signature characteristic of a DO is the **cloud of do-mites** that fly into and out of openings on the partivants.


**DO-Mite Characteristics:**

Physical Properties:

- Size: 0.5-2.0 mm (visible as individuals with magnification)

- Morphology: Winged, insect-like body plan

- Flight capability: Active powered flight, 5-15 kph

- Lifespan: 6-48 hours (variable by type)

- Production: Continuous from specialized DO-mite production partivants

Functional Types:

- Hormonal carriers: Transport emotional/arousal state information

- Neurological carriers: Transport decision/command information

- Memory carriers: Transport learned information, spatial maps

- Sensory carriers: Transport processed sensory data

- Metabolic carriers: Transport nutrient/health status information

Behavioral Patterns:

- Signature DO behavior: Visible clouds entering/exiting partivant openings

- Traffic flow: Bidirectional (information exchange, not broadcast)

- Density indicates activity: Heavy traffic = high cognitive load

- Directional patterns: Targeted flights between specific partivants

Information Encoding:

- Chemical payload: Protein-based information molecules

- Delivery mechanism: DO-mite lands, enters partivant opening, releases payload

- Reception: Partivant absorbs chemicals, processes information

- Response: Partivant may generate return DO-mites with response information

**Communication Latency:**

Local exchange (partivants within 50m):

- DO-mite flight time: 10-30 seconds

- Information processing: 30-60 seconds

- Total latency: 1-2 minutes for command-response cycle

Medium-range exchange (50-200m):

- DO-mite flight time: 1-3 minutes

- Information processing: 30-60 seconds

- Total latency: 2-5 minutes for command-response cycle

Long-range exchange (200-500m):

- DO-mite flight time: 3-10 minutes

- Information processing: 30-60 seconds 

- Total latency: 5-15 minutes for command-response cycle
