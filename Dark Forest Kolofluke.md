# Dark Forest - Kolofluke

**Location:** Deep valleys of [[14e-Kolofluke|Kolofluke]], central mountain spine  
**Climate:** Cool, humid, perpetually dark  
**Light Level:** Near-zero (blocked by [[Flying-Forest|Flying Forest]])  
**Key Feature:** [[Note-on-Kolofluke-rDO|Radio-based DOs]] dominant, minimal photosynthesis  
**Unique:** Biological darkness adaptation, electromagnetic communication

---

## Overview

### The Sunless Biome

**The Dark Forest on Kolofluke** is one result of the [[Flying-Forest|Flying Forest]]. Where the flying forest intersects with the cloud forest of Kolo, the steep mountain valleys get **zero sunlight** and have evolved unique adaptations for the darkness.

**Formation:**
- [[Flying-Forest|Flying Forest]] concentrates above mountains
- Active volcanic zone
- Permanent dense layer blocks sunlight
- Deep valleys receive no direct light
- Year-round darkness (not seasonal)
- Ash/Cloud layer + Flying Forest = double barrier

**Geographic Extent:**
- Central spine valleys of [[14e-Kolofluke|Kolofluke]]
- 50-100 valleys (each 5-20 km long)
- Valley depths: 500-1500m below ridges
- Steep walls (60-80° slopes typical)
- Total area: ~50,000 km² (patchy distribution)

---

## Light Environment

### Near-Total Darkness

**Illumination Sources:**
- **[[13b-Lumna-Secondary-Moon|Lumna]]:** Faint reflected light (0.01% surface level)
- **Bioluminescence:** Organisms generate own light (minimal)
- **Sky glow:** Diffuse scatter from above (negligible)
- **Effective:** Photosynthesis impossible

**Organism Adaptations:**
- Eyes reduced or absent (vision useless)
- [[42c-rDO-Duskmantle-Hunter|Echolocation]] primary (acoustic mapping)
- Chemical sensing enhanced (pheromone tracking)
- [[Note-on-Kolofluke-rDO|Radio communication]] evolved (rDO organisms)
- Tactile sensing (whiskers, sensitive skin)

---

## Energy Sources

### No Photosynthesis

**Primary Production:**
- **[[8E-Autotrophy|Chemosynthesis]]:** Oxidizing inorganic compounds
  - Sulfur compounds (volcanic vents)
  - Methane seeps (geological)
  - Ammonia oxidation (decomposition)
- **Saprotrophy:** Decomposing organic matter
  - Material falls from above (dead Flying Forest)
  - Terrestrial runoff (washed down slopes)
  - Animal waste and corpses

**Limited Productivity:**
- Much lower than photosynthetic ecosystems
- ~15% of surface productivity
- Supports smaller biomass
- Slower ecological processes

## Dominant Organisms

### rDO Apex

**[[Note-on-Kolofluke-rDO|Radio-Based Distributed Organisms (rDOs)]]:**
- Primary large fauna
- Use electromagnetic signals (darkness-independent)
- [[Distributed-Organisms-Overview|Distributed bodies]] coordinate via radio
- Examples: [[rDO-Duskmantle-Stalker|rDO-Duskmantle Stalker]]

**Why rDOs Dominate:**
- [[CEP-and-Do-Mites|Do-mites]] need light (cannot navigate in darkness)
- Radio waves penetrate darkness
- Longer range than chemical signals
- Faster coordination than chemicals
- **Evolutionary advantage:** Only viable communication system
- Probably evolved from cave dwelling forms

## Vegetation (Limited)

### Non-Photosynthetic "Plants"

**Chemosynthetic Autotrophs:**
- Near volcanic vents (sulfur oxidizers)
- Near methane seeps (methane oxidizers)
- Sessile or very slow-moving
- Dark coloration (no pigments needed)

**Saprotrophic Organisms:**
- Decompose fallen material
- Fungal-analogs (filamentous networks)
- Feed on dead Flying Forest organisms
- Feed on animal waste
- Critical nutrient recyclers

**"Ghost Trees" (Saprophytes):**
- Tall (5-10m) pale structures
- No photosynthetic tissue
- Absorb nutrients from soil (decomposed matter)
- Reproduce via spores (wind-dispersed upward, exit valley)
- Provide structure (climbing substrate for others)

---

## Herbivore Analogs

### Detritivores and Browsers

**Detritus Feeders:**
- Consume fallen organic matter
- Material from Flying Forest (primary source)
- Terrestrial runoff (secondary)
- Nocturnal on surface (irrelevant here—always dark)

**"Herbivores" (Actually Omnivores):**
- Browse chemosynthetic organisms
- Eat fungal networks
- Consume decomposing material
- Supplement with insects/small animals
- [[Trophic-Roles-No-Plant-Animal|No true herbivores]] (insufficient plant biomass)

**rDO-Shadowbrowser:**
- [[Distributed-Organisms-Overview|Distributed organism]]
- Feeding partivants graze saprophytes
- Scout partivants detect food patches (radio coordination)
- Cognitive partivants coordinate in darkness (radio signals)

## Predators

### Ambush and Acoustic Hunters

**[[42c-rDO-Duskmantle-Hunter|rDO-Duskmantle Hunter]]:**
- Apex predator
- [[Note-on-Kolofluke-rDO|Radio communication]] between [[Partivant-Definition|partivants]]
- Echolocation (active sound pulses)
- Infrared sensing (detect body heat)
- Coordinated ambush (radio-synchronized)
- Hunt in complete darkness (no visual cues)

**Smaller Predators:**
- Solitary hunters (standard composites)
- Enhanced hearing (passive listening)
- Chemical tracking (pheromone following)
- Opportunistic (scavenge as well as hunt)

**Hunting Strategies:**
- Ambush (wait silently near game trails)
- Acoustic tracking (follow prey sounds)
- Thermal tracking (infrared pits)
- Coordinated drives (rDOs only—radio coordination)

## Sensory Adaptationsn

### Acoustic Vision

**Active Echolocation:**
- Ultrasonic pulses (20-80 kHz)
- Echo processing in [[33e-Sensory-Integration|SENS brain]]
- Three-dimensional terrain mapping
- Prey detection (movement, breathing)
- Shared via radio ([[42c-rDO-Duskmantle-Hunter|rDO-Duskmantle]]—all partivants "see" data)

**Advantages:**
- Works in complete darkness
- Penetrates vegetation (to degree)
- Detects movement (Doppler shift)
- Continuous environmental awareness

**Limitations:**
- Energy expensive (constant emission)
- Reveals location (predator detection)
- Cluttered environments confusing (many echoes)
- Range limited (~100m typical)

---

### Darkness Specializations

**Vision (Reduced/Absent):**
- Eyes vestigial (some organisms)
- Or absent entirely (others)
- Energy saved (no visual processing)
- [[42c-rDO-Duskmantle-Hunter|rDO-Duskmantle]] nearly blind

**Hearing (Enhanced):**
- Massive ear structures (parabolic collectors)
- Frequency range extended (infrasonic to ultrasonic)
- Directional accuracy high
- Primary sensory mode

**Chemical Sensing (Enhanced):**
- Olfactory organs enlarged
- Pheromone detection sensitive
- Taste receptors abundant
- Trail-following common

**Tactile (Enhanced):**
- Whiskers/vibrissae common
- Sensitive skin (pressure, vibration)
- Lateral line analogs (some aquatic)

**Infrared:**
- **Infrared pits:** Detect body heat (thermal imaging)
- **[[Note-on-Kolofluke-rDO|Radio reception]]:** Electromagnetic communication (rDOs)
- **Magnetic sense:** Navigation in darkness

## Nutrient Cycling

### Import-Dependent

**Input Sources:**
1. **Fallen Flying Forest organisms** (primary)
   - Dead autotrophs drift down
   - Decompose on forest floor
   - Provide carbon, nutrients

2. **Terrestrial runoff** (secondary)
   - Rain washes material from ridges
   - Soil particles, organic matter
   - Streams carry nutrients downslope

3. **Animal waste**
   - Predator scat, prey remains
   - Decomposition releases nutrients
   - Recycled within ecosystem

**Internal Cycling:**
- Decomposers break down inputs
- Chemosynthetic autotrophs fix carbon (minimal)
- Herbivore-analogs consume decomposers/saprophytes
- Predators eat herbivore-analogs
- Death returns nutrients to decomposers

**Export:**
- Minimal (closed system mostly)
- Some organisms exit valleys (reproduce outside)
- Water runoff (carries dissolved nutrients downslope to coast)

## Climate

### Cool and Humid

**Temperature:**
- Cool year-round (5-15°C)
- Stable (no direct sunlight = minimal diurnal variation)
- Inversions common (cold air pools in valleys)
- Frost rare (humidity prevents)

**Humidity:**
- Very high (90-100% relative humidity)
- Fog/mist perpetual (cloud layer in valleys)
- Dripping canopy (condensation constant)
- Water abundant (streams, seeps)

**Wind:**
- Minimal (sheltered by valley walls)
- Occasional down-slope flows (cold air drainage)
- Stagnant air (low circulation)

---

## Terrain

### Steep and Complex

**Valley Morphology:**
- Narrow (1-3 km width typical)
- Deep (500-1500m below ridges)
- Steep walls (60-80° slopes)
- V-shaped cross-section (stream-cut)

**Substrate:**
- Rocky outcrops (exposed bedrock)
- Thin soils (limited plant growth)
- Accumulated organic matter (valley bottoms)
- Unstable slopes (rockfall, landslides)

**Water:**
- Streams (fed by ridge precipitation)
- Seeps (groundwater emerges)
- Pools (slow-moving sections)
- Waterfalls (steep gradient)

## Evolution and Adaptation

### Why This System Evolved

**Environmental Pressure:**
- [[Flying-Forest|Flying Forest]] blocked sunlight permanently
- [[CEP-and-Do-Mites|Chemical communication]] failed (do-mites need light)
- Selection for alternative strategies

**[[Note-on-Kolofluke-rDO|Radio Communication Evolution]]:**
1. Pre-adaptation (weak [[8F-Electrogenesis|electrogenesis]] for prey detection)
2. Enhanced signals (stronger transmission selected)
3. Dedicated organs ([[8F-Electrogenesis|electrogenesis partifuncts]])
4. Relay infrastructure (signal amplification)
5. Full [[Distributed-Organisms-Overview|distributed organism]] coordination

**Timeline:**
- Flying Forest established: ~10-15 million years ago
- Dark Forest formed: Shortly after
- rDO evolution: 5-10 million years ago
- Rapid adaptation enabled by [[Non-Homochirality|modular biology]]

## Population Dynamics

### Low Density, Specialized

**Biomass:**
- Much lower than surface ecosystems
- ~5-10% of equivalent photosynthetic system
- Limited by energy input (chemosynthesis, detritus)

**Species Diversity:**
- Moderate (specialized niches)
- High endemism (isolated valleys)
- Many species found nowhere else
- [[Note-on-Kolofluke-rDO|rDOs unique]] to Kolofluke

**Population Sizes:**
- Small (energy-limited)
- Low reproductive rates
- Long lifespans (slow metabolism)
- [[42c-rDO-Duskmantle-Hunter|rDO-Duskmantle]]: 0.3-1.0 packs per 100 km²

---

## Ecological Interactions

### Simplified Food Webs

**Trophic Structure:**
- **Decomposers/Chemosynthesizers:** Base (limited productivity)
- **Detritivores/Browsers:** Primary consumers
- **Small predators:** Mesopredators
- **rDO apex predators:** Top (low density)

**Simplified vs. Surface:**
- Fewer trophic levels (energy-limited)
- Less species diversity
- More omnivory (flexible feeding)
- Longer food chains impossible (insufficient energy)

- Remote (far from bases)
- Weather (fog, rain constant)

**Observation:**

- Equipment failures (humidity, darkness)

**Understanding:**

- Population estimates uncertain (low density, hidden)
- Ecological interactions poorly known
- [[42c-rDO-Duskmantle-Hunter|Coordinated hunting]] difficult to observe
