# Digestive Systems

**Architecture:** Through-gut (mouth → anus) most common  
**Key Feature:** Digestive partifuncts specialized by diet  
**Variation:** Simple to complex, single to multiple mouths  
**Universal:** Everything is an omnivore (no huge protein difference across life)

---

### Through-Gut Configuration

**Most Common Pattern:**
- **Mouth:** Intake opening (anterior)
- **Digestive tract:** Processing tube
- **Anus:** Waste expulsion (posterior)
- Linear flow, one direction

**Mouth Aids:**
Common organisms have pedipalps or trunks to aid in bringing food to mouth, or mouths at the ends of trunks.

**Variation:**
- Some organisms have **two mouths**
- Multiple intake points
- Specialized mouths for different food types
- Rare: Circular/radial feeding structures

## Digestive Partifuncts

### Genetic Lineages

**DG (Digestive) Family:**
Each digestive system is a [[Partifunct Core Definition|partifunct]] with own [[GFP-and-Genodules|GFP]], not shared with rest of organism.

**Common Types:**
- **DGV-HERB**: Herbivore digestive tract (cellulose breakdown)
- **DGV-CARN**: Carnivore digestive tract (protein digestion)
- **DGV-OMNI**: Omnivore digestive tract (general purpose)
- **DGV-FILT**: Filter feeder (plankton extraction)

**[[Multi-Expression-Potential|Multi-Expression]]:**
- **In autotrophs:** Root systems (soil nutrient absorption)
- **In herbivores:** Complex gut (cellulose digestion)
- **In carnivores:** Simple gut (protein digestion)
- **In filter feeders:** Straining structures (particle extraction)

## Everything is an Omnivore

### No Huge Protein Difference

There is no huge difference between the sets of proteins found in 'plants' vs. those in 'animals':
- Lignin and calcium both used for bones and tree trunks
- Chitin used for plant bodies as well as insect bodies
- Many creatures are omnivorous

**Implications:**
- Most organisms **can** digest both "plant" and "animal" matter
- Specialization is behavioral/ecological, not strictly chemical
- [[41-Trophic-Roles-No-Plant-Animal|No clear plant vs animal divide]]
- Digestive flexibility common

## Mouth Structures are seperate partifuncts

### Jaws and Teeth

**Jaw Types:**
- **Iris-toothed mouth:** Circular arrangement, radial closing
- **Hinged jaws:** Similar to Earth carnivores
- **Grinding plates:** For processing tough plant matter
- **Filter structures:** Baleen-like for aquatic feeders

**Tooth Analogs:**
- Lignin-based "teeth" (most common)
- Calcium-based teeth (some lineages)
- Continuously growing (wear-resistant)
- Variable arrangement by diet

## Digestive Tract Complexity

### Simple Systems

**Carnivore Pattern:**
- Short digestive tract
- High acid concentration
- Rapid protein breakdown
- Quick nutrient absorption
- Simple tube, minimal processing

**Small Organism Pattern:**
- Minimal gut complexity
- Rapid throughput
- Surface-area-limited digestion
- May lack specialized regions

---

### Complex Systems

**Herbivore Pattern:**
- Long, convoluted digestive tract
- Multiple chambers (stomach analogs)
- Fermentation chambers with symbiotic microbes
- Slow processing (cellulose difficult to break down)
- Complex compartmentalization

**Large Organism Pattern:**
- Specialized regions (crop, stomach, intestines)
- Chemical differentiation (acid regions, alkaline regions)
- Extensive surface area (villi, folds)
- Enzyme diversity

---

## Nutrient Absorption

### Interface with Circulatory System

**Transfer Mechanisms:**
Shared organs, where the capillaries of two partifuncts infiltrate to within a single membrane of each other, are used to pass blood sugar (and other nutrients).

**Absorption Sites:**
- Intestinal walls rich in [[34c-Transfer-Organs|transfer organs]]
- Nutrients diffuse into blood
- [[Circulatory-Systems|Circulatory systems]] distribute throughout body
- Each partifunct receives appropriate fuel

---

## Symbiotic Microbes

### Fermentation Chambers

**Herbivore Specialization:**
- Large fermentation chambers in gut
- House microbial communities
- Break down cellulose and other complex carbohydrates
- Produce usable nutrients as byproduct

**Microbial Origin:**
- May be [[60-Microbiosphere-Overview|S-BLU or F-BLU organisms]]
- Collected from environment (food, water)
- Established during early life
- Maintained throughout adulthood

---

## Waste Processing

### Excretion

**Waste Products:**
- Undigested material (feces)
- Metabolic byproducts
- Excess salts, toxins

**Excretory Organs:**
- Some organisms have specialized waste-processing partifuncts
- Others use digestive tract exclusively
- [[34c-Transfer-Organs|Transfer organs]] remove waste from blood
- Concentrated before expulsion

**eGFP in Waste:**
All waste products (feces, urine) contain [[Environmental-GFP|eGFP]] from consumed organisms, contributing to environmental genetic pool.

---

## Energy Storage

### Reserves and Buffers

**Fat Analogs:**
- Energy storage in specialized tissues
- May be part of digestive partifunct
- Or separate storage partifuncts
- Mobilized during scarcity

**Glycogen Analogs:**
- Short-term energy buffer
- Stored in muscles and organs
- Rapid access during activity

## Feeding Strategies

### By Trophic Role

**Autotrophs:**
- [[8E-Autotrophy|Photosynthetic organs]] or root-like nutrient absorption
- "Digestive" system = soil processing or atmospheric intake
- Some mobile autotrophs supplement with heterotrophy

**Herbivores (Grazers/Browsers):**
- Complex digestive systems
- Fermentation chambers
- Long processing time
- Continuous feeding often needed

**Carnivores:**
- Simple, efficient digestion
- Gorge feeding common (large meals, long intervals)
- High-energy diet
- Rapid processing

**Filter Feeders:**
- Strain water or air for particles
- Large intake, selective retention
- Continuous feeding
- Often sessile or slow-moving

**Omnivores (Most Common):**
- Flexible digestive capabilities
- Variable diet by availability
- Moderate complexity
- Opportunistic feeding

## Environmental Cultivation

### Farming Behavior

Some organisms actively manage their food sources:

**[[72d-Environmental-Cultivation|Environmental Cultivation]]:**
- Grazers promote growth of preferred grasses
- Predators maintain prey populations
- Creates feedback loop
- Enhances reproduction (collects preferred [[Environmental-GFP|eGFP]])

## Variable Configurations

### Organism-Specific Patterns

**Simple Organisms:**
- Direct gut tube
- Minimal processing
- Fast throughput

**Complex Mobile Organisms:**
- Specialized regions
- Multiple stomach chambers (some)
- Extensive intestines
- Symbiotic partnerships

**[[50-Distributed-Organisms-Overview|Distributed Organisms]]:**
- **[[52-DO-ScrubDeer-Browser|DO-ScrubDeer]]:** Feeding nodes collect, digestive nodes (fermenters) process
- Specialized partivants for different digestive stages
- Nutrient distribution via [[51-CEP-and-Do-Mites|DO-mites]]

**[[55-Combinant-Behaviors|Combinant Organisms]]:**
- **[[Stringbirds]]:** Individual birds have incomplete digestive tracts
- Join mouth-to-anus to create complete system
- Pass food through multiple bodies for processing

## Development

### Womb Assembly

**Digestive Formation:**
- Digestive partifzygote develops independently
- [[3B-Womb-Partifunct-Systems|Womb]] coordinates gut tube formation
- Mouth and anus placement determined
- Connection to circulatory system established
- Must be functional at birth

**Diet Adaptation:**
- Womb may select digestive type based on environment
- Herbivore gut if plants abundant
- Carnivore gut if prey abundant
- Omnivore gut as generalist strategy

---

## Evolutionary Flexibility

### Rapid Dietary Shifts

**Horizontal Transfer:**
- New digestive partifunct acquired via [[Environmental-GFP|eGFP]]
- Population can shift diet in single generation
- Example: Herbivore population acquires carnivore gut
- Enables rapid niche exploitation

**Selective Pressure:**
- Food availability drives digestive evolution
- Successful digestive strategies spread rapidly
- [[Natural-Selection-on-TPIV|Multi-level selection]] on diet and digestion
- Cultural behaviors (food preferences) affect genetic success
