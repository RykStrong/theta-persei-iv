# The Hexagirdle and Octagirdle

The hexagirdle (six-limbed) and octagirdle (eight-limbed) represent TPIV's most common structural body plans—ring-shaped skeletal frameworks supporting radially arranged limbs. These modular partifuncts form the organism's core, housing central nerve cords and major circulatory pathways within protective channels. Construction varies dramatically: some organisms possess rigid, armored girdles providing structural stability; others utilize flexible, articulated designs enabling lateral undulation or compression. Many exhibit partial rigidity. 
The same hexagirdle partifunct might express as heavily armored in terrestrial predators, lightweight and flexible in aerial organisms, or hydrodynamically streamlined in aquatic species, demonstrating how single genetic modules adapt morphologically across diverse ecological roles.
