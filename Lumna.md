# Lumna - Secondary Moon

 Lumna (Secondary Moon - "Weekly Modulator")
    . Orbit: ~530,000 km
    . Period: 37.2  local days
    . Size: 0.15 R☾ (~520 km diameter, high albedo icy crust)
    . Mass: 0.5 M☾
    . Notes: Prograde orbit. Creates predictable spring/neap tidal cycle every 7 days 
      (quarter-phase intervals). When aligned with Tida, produces enhanced tides 
      (15-20m); when perpendicular, produces neap tides (7-12m). Highly reflective 
      surface provides significant nighttime illumination.


---

## Physical Characteristics

**Size & Composition:**
- Half the mass of Earth's moon
- Radius: ~1,200 km
- Highly reflective surface (albedo ~0.65)
- Possibly icy or light-colored mineral composition
- Tidally locked to TPIV

**Appearance from TPIV:**
- Brilliant white to silver
- Smaller angular size than [Tida](Tida.md)
- **Exceptionally bright** - comparable to full Earth moon × 2
- Easily visible even during day
- Distinct phases over 37-day cycle

---

## Nighttime Illumination

**Brightness:**
- Bright enough to read by at full phase
- Creates distinctive **silver-blue night sky**
- Casts clear shadows
- Enables visual activity at night

**Biological Impact:**
- Supports nocturnal visual organisms
- Reduces predation pressure during bright nights
- Some organisms show lunar-synchronized behaviors
- Enables extended feeding/activity periods

**Sky Appearance:**
- Full Lumna: Brilliant silver light
- Crescent Lumna: Still noticeably bright
- Combined with [Tida](Tida.md): "Double moon" nights
- When both full: Nearly twilight conditions

---

## Tidal Effects

**Secondary Tidal Force:**
- Weaker than Tida (more distant, smaller mass)
- Creates ~2-3 meter contribution to tides
- 37-day tidal cycle overlays Tida's 6-day cycle

**Tidal Interactions:**

**Aligned with Tida (same side):**
- Forces combine
- **Super-tides:** 15+ meter range
- Occurs every ~47 days
- Most extreme tidal events on TPIV

**Opposed to Tida (opposite sides):**
- Forces partially cancel
- **Neap tides:** 5-8 meter range
- Calmer coastal conditions
- Some organisms time reproduction to neap tides

**Complex Patterns:**
- Never perfectly synchronized with Tida (different periods)
- Creates non-repeating tidal calendar
- Organisms adapt to variable conditions
- Favors flexible [modular biology](Partifunct%20Core%20Definition.md)

## Sky Appearance

**Phases:**
- **Full Lumna:** Brilliant silver disk, bright night
- **Half Lumna:** Still very bright, distinct shadows
- **Crescent Lumna:** Visible as bright sliver
- **New Lumna:** Darkest nights (with new Tida)

**Combined with Tida:**
- Both full: Nearly twilight conditions
- One full, one new: Moderate illumination
- Both new: True darkness (rare)
- Crescent + crescent: Beautiful "double crescent"
