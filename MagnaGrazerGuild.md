**Functional Class:** Large Hexapedal Grazers of the Magna Central Steppe

**Dominant Trophic Role(s):** Primary herbivore / bulk biomass converters

## 1. Guild Overview

The **Magna Steppe Grazer Guild** comprises the dominant large-bodied herbivores of the Magna central plains and Central Lakes region. Members share a broadly similar role: converting dense steppe and littoral autotroph biomass into mobile, predator-accessible form, while engineering soil structure and nutrient cycling at landscape scale.

Although guild members occupy distinct **taxa peaks** (Zebra Grazer, Buffalo Grazer, Sprint Grazer, Hippo Grazer, Hog Grazer, etc.), they are not separated by hard species boundaries. Instead, each taxon represents a **high-density cluster** in morphospace, with thinner but real occupancy between peaks. This gives the guild a �lumpy continuum� structure: fewer forms and individuals between the named taxa than Earth biologists would expect, but those intermediates do exist.

**Key Shared Characteristics**

- Typically **hexapodal**, built around a **hexagirdle** body frame with six locomotor limbs.  
- One or more **tentacle partifuncts** for sensory elevation and/or food manipulation.  
- Large, multi-chambered grazer digestive stacks specialized on SSRS-A �Grasses� and associated steppe autotroph guilds.  
- Womb-enabled assembly, with a strong **�large-bodied plains grazer� scaffold bias** in RF-SCF lineages.  
- Social, herd-forming behavior with herd-level vigilance and migration.

## 2. Biome & Environmental Context

**Primary Biomes**

- **Magna Central Grasslands**  
  - Semi-arid to sub-humid, deep soils, dominated by SSRS-A �Grasses� and Brownflower Shrubs.  
  - Strong seasonal swings in temperature and precipitation; periodic droughts.

- **Central Lakes Littoral and Floodplain**  
  - Seasonally flooded margins of the Central Tidal Lakes complex.  
  - Mixed grass, reed, and wandering-flora mosaics.

- **Shrub�Steppe Ecotones**  
  - Transitional zones where grazers overlap with browser guilds and root-feeder guilds.

**Seasonal Dynamics**

The guild tracks the **Magna Central Lakes seasonal cycle**:

- Wet season: herds concentrate on lake margins and floodplains, exploiting lush regrowth.  
- Dry/cold season: herds retreat to more stable grass plains, following deep-rooted grasses and conserved soil moisture.  
- Longer-period oscillations (e.g. Radita-driven radiation cycles) act as coarse chronobiological cues but do not strongly differentiate guild members.

For climate detail see:

## 3. Guild Morphospace & Metrics

PSV and MDI are defined **per taxon** (see Section 4). At guild scale we consider:

- The **range** of PSV/MDI across member taxa.  
- How those taxa cluster in Partifunct Expression Graph (PEG) space.  
- Which taxa form the �center� vs the morphological/functional extremes of the guild.

### 3.1 Partifunct Statistics at Guild Level

Across named grazer taxa, certain partifunct lineages recur:

- **Structural:**  
  - `ST-BDY-GIRD-HEXAS/HEXAF` � hexagirdle body frame.  
  - `ST-LMB-SEGM-L3T3B` � material variants � primary limb segments.  
  - `ST-LMB-TENT-*` � dorsal sensor and/or feeding tentacles.  
  - `ST-END-BLNT-*` � hooves, multi-toe blunt terminals.

- **Digestive:**  
  - `DG-GRD`, `DG-FRC`, `DG-ABS`, `DG-REP` � classic grazer grinder�fermenter�absorber�repacker chain.  

- **Circulatory/Respiratory:**  
  - Multiple `CR-HRT` units, many small `CR-LNG` clusters, `CR-FLT` and `CR-VAL` lineages.

- **Control & Sensory:**  
  - `SN-OPT-EYE-COMP`, `SN-OPT-EYS-*`, `SN-AUR`, `SN-OLC`, `SN-MAG`, `SN-RAD`.  
  - `CN-MOT`, `CN-SEN`, `CN-NAV`, `CN-TRT`, `CN-MEM`, `CN-SOC`.

These form the **common partifunct pool** of the guild; individual TPMs track counts and lineage variants.

### 3.2 PSV / MDI Envelope

- **Typical Taxon-Level PSV Range:**  
  - ~**78�88%** across major named taxa.  
  - Most guild taxa share a high-consistency core set (hexagirdle, six limb chains, grazer gut), with variation in tentacles, terminals, armor, and adjunct organs.

- **Typical Taxon-Level MDI Range:**  
  - ~**2.4�4.1**.  
  - Shape variation is moderate to marked: body depth, limb length ratios, terminal structure, and semi-aquatic adaptations differ between taxa.

**Guild Morphospace**

In PEG space the guild appears as a **constellation of dense taxon clouds**:

- **Zebra Grazer** sits near the **center**: medium-large, generalized steppe grazer.  
- **Buffalo Grazer** pushes toward **higher mass and robustness** (more structural and circulatory investment).  
- **Sprint Grazer** extends toward **cursorial extremes** (longer limbs, higher heart density, lighter gut).  
- **Hippo Grazer** occupies the **semi-aquatic corner** (modified terminals, denser integument, buoyancy tweaks).  
- **Hog Grazer** leans toward **root- and detritus-feeding** (forelimb emphasis, stronger cranial/foregut modules).

Between these peaks are thinner but real populations of **intermediate morphs**, only some of which are named taxa.

## 4. Representative Taxa (Guild Members)

> Anatomical detail for each taxon is in its TPM; this section is functional and comparative.

### 4.1 Zebra Grazer

- **Functional Label:** `GW-L` � Grazer, Walker � Large (hexapedal)  
- **Key TPM:** [TPM Example](TPM%20Example.md)  
- **PSV / MDI (Taxon-Level):**  
  - PSV ? **83%** (�1?)  
  - MDI ? **2.8**  

**Typ. Ecological Slot**

- The **most generalized** plains grazer of the Magna steppe.  
- Forms the �center of mass� for the guild: abundant, wide-ranging, and diet-flexible within the SSRS-A grass layer.

**Morphological Theme**

- Hexagirdle with six stout legs; medium-deep trunk.  
- One dorsal **sensor tentacle** carrying the main sensory cluster.  
- One anterior **feeding tentacle** ending in a toothed grasper, delivering cut grass to a body-front mouth.  
- High-contrast striping over body and tentacles.

**Behavioral Theme**

- Medium to large mixed herds; strong herd-level vigilance via elevated sensor tentacles.  
- Bi-directional walking, but fast running only stable in **mouth-front mode**.  
- Classic �flush-and-flee� response to pack hunters: tightly coordinated, high-speed displacement.

**Within-Guild Relationships**

- Sets the **baseline** against which other grazer taxa are interpreted.  
- Shares mixed herds or overlapping ranges with Sprint Grazer and Hog Grazer in some regions.

### 4.2 Buffalo Grazer

- **Functional Label:** `GW-H` � Grazer, Walker � Heavy  
- **Key TPM:** [MagnaGrazerGuild](MagnaGrazerGuild.md) (planned/placeholder)  
- **PSV / MDI (Taxon-Level):**  
  - PSV ? **88%**  
  - MDI ? **2.4**  

**Typ. Ecological Slot**

- High-mass, high-momentum grazer specialized for **dense sward and shrub-edge** grazing.  
- Occupies wetter or more productive zones where biomass supports larger bodies.

**Morphological Theme**

- Heavier hexagirdle, thicker limb segments (more `ST-LMB-SEGM-*` per limb, with more calcium-biased variants).  
- Deeper trunk with enlarged fermentation chambers.  
- Shorter, sturdier sensor tentacle; feeding tentacle robust but less flexible than Zebra Grazer�s.

**Behavioral Theme**

- Forms tighter, denser herds that rely more on **stand-and-intimidate** or short rushes than prolonged flight.  
- Often takes the **front ranks** when herds share space with Zebra Grazers.  
- Less migratory; tracks stable high-biomass zones.

**Within-Guild Relationships**

- Competes with Zebra Grazer where forage is rich; tends to dominate **prime patches**.  
- Its stable body plan and high PSV make it a **conservative taxon anchor** on the heavy-mass edge of the guild.

### 4.3 Sprint Grazer

- **Functional Label:** `GW-LF` � Grazer, Walker � Light / Fast  
- **Key TPM:** [MagnaGrazerGuild](MagnaGrazerGuild.md) (planned/placeholder)  
- **PSV / MDI (Taxon-Level):**  
  - PSV ? **78%**  
  - MDI ? **4.1**  

**Typ. Ecological Slot**

- High-speed, lighter-bodied grazer specialized for **patchy or predator-exposed** habitats.  
- Often feeds on slightly taller or more scattered grasses than Zebra Grazer.

**Morphological Theme**

- More elongated distal limb segments; lower overall body mass.  
- Slimmer trunk, somewhat reduced fermentation volume.  
- Sensor tentacle typically long and narrow, optimizing horizon scanning.  
- Feeding tentacle finer, with more dexterous grasper terminals for selective foraging.

**Behavioral Theme**

- Smaller herds or bands; looser spacing, high mobility.  
- Relies on **early detection and outrunning** of pack hunters rather than mass defense.  
- More pronounced seasonal movements; often the first grazer taxon to exploit newly greening areas.

**Within-Guild Relationships**

- Occupies the **cursorial extreme** of the guild morphospace.  
- Frequently forms **outer halo** around Zebra Grazer herds, acting as both grazing vanguard and early-warning fringe.

### 4.4 Hippo Grazer

- **Functional Label:** `GSW-L` � Grazer, Swimmer�Walker � Large  
- **Key TPM:** [MagnaGrazerGuild](MagnaGrazerGuild.md) (planned/placeholder)  
- **PSV / MDI (Taxon-Level):**  
  - PSV ? **81%**  
  - MDI ? **3.7**  

**Typ. Ecological Slot**

- Semi-aquatic grazer of **lake margins and slow channels** around the Central Lakes.  
- Feeds on submerged and emergent grasses/reeds, with occasional forays onto shore.

**Morphological Theme**

- Limbs with broadened, partially webbed terminals; denser skin and subdermal tissue.  
- Some limb segments biased toward chitin or composite materials for impact and abrasion in muck and water.  
- Sensor tentacle shorter and more armored, but capable of periscope-like elevation above water surface.  
- Body mass more compact, with shifted buoyancy and CR-RES reservoirs suited to immersion.

**Behavioral Theme**

- Spends long periods standing or moving in shallow water, surfacing sensor tentacle and nostril clusters.  
- Flee responses are typically **into deeper water** rather than onto open land.  
- Aggregations are smaller and more territorial than plains-herd grazers.

**Within-Guild Relationships**

- Forms an **edge taxon** between steppe grazers and wetland-specialist guilds.  
- Seasonal overlap with plains grazers at receding shorelines; may share feeding fronts with Hog Grazers around exposed mud and roots.

---

### 4.5 Hog Grazer

- **Functional Label:** `GW-R` � Grazer / Root-Feeder � Walker  
- **Key TPM:** [MagnaGrazerGuild](MagnaGrazerGuild.md) (planned/placeholder)  
- **PSV / MDI (Taxon-Level):**  
  - PSV ? **80%**  
  - MDI ? **3.3**  

**Typ. Ecological Slot**

- Mixed grazer�digger specialized on **roots, tubers, and rhizomes**, plus low vegetation.  
- Favors patchy grass�shrub mosaics, floodplain edges, and trampled areas.

**Morphological Theme**

- Forelimbs and anterior limb segments more powerful; terminals adapted for scraping and digging.  
- Cranial/feeding tentacle and mouth complex reinforced for rooting and prying.  
- Gut incorporates more robust `DG-GRD` and `DG-DGS` (saprotrophic and soil-organic processing) modules.

**Behavioral Theme**

- Smaller groups or family clusters, often spreading around larger herds of Zebra or Buffalo Grazers.  
- Frequently reworks soil surface, creating disturbed patches that alter plant guild composition.  
- Manages higher toxin load, reflecting a more opportunistic diet.

**Within-Guild Relationships**

- Functionally complements pure grazers by exploiting a **different vertical band of the soil�plant profile**.  
- Acts as a **keystone bioturbator**, creating microsites that benefit both grasses and certain browser/autotroph taxa.

### 4.6 Minor / Intermediate Forms

Between these major taxa, field surveys note several less common or more localized morphs:

- **Crimson Strider** � lightly built, long-legged grazer of drier upland fringes.  
- **Pale Sprint Grazer** � high-speed, pale-patterned form associated with very open, predator-exposed flats.  
- **Dune Grazer** (provisional) � suspected taxon adapted to sandy substrates with broad, low-pressure terminals.

These forms occupy **intermediate positions** in PEG space between the main taxa, reinforcing that the guild is a continuum with peaks rather than isolated �species islands.�

## 5. Intra-Guild Structure

### 5.1 Niche Partitioning

Partitioning occurs along several axes:

- **Body Mass & Height**  
  - Buffalo Grazer > Zebra Grazer > Hog Grazer ? Hippo Grazer > Sprint Grazer.  
- **Dietary Focus**  
  - Zebra/Buffalo: bulk mid-height grasses.  
  - Sprint: patchy or higher grasses, often in early regrowth.  
  - Hippo: submerged/emergent vegetation.  
  - Hog: roots, rhizomes, disturbed-soil vegetation.  
- **Microhabitat**  
  - Core plains (Zebra, Buffalo).  
  - Ecotone and disturbance edges (Hog, Sprint).  
  - Shoreline and shallows (Hippo).

### 5.2 Temporal Partitioning

- **Daily**  
  - Zebra/Buffalo: strong crepuscular peaks.  
  - Sprint: more diurnal flexibility, including hot mid-day movement in windy conditions.  
  - Hippo: more nocturnal shoreline foraging.  
  - Hog: variable, often exploiting times when heavier grazers are elsewhere.

- **Seasonal**  
  - Sprint and Zebra often **lead migrations**, tracking new growth.  
  - Buffalo lag slightly, concentrating where growth is densest.  
  - Hippo tracks water-line movements.  
  - Hog follows disturbance and soil softening patterns more than calendar season per se.

### 5.3 Social & Reproductive Patterns

- Large, mixed-age herds are typical for Zebra and Buffalo Grazers.  
- Sprint Grazers form smaller, more fluid bands.  
- Hippo and Hog forms trend toward **smaller, more territorial groupings**.  
- Womb assembly cycles are broadly synchronized at guild level with the **Magna seasonal cycle**, but precise timing can differ by taxon and region.

## 6. Inter-Guild Relationships

### 6.1 Predator Guild: Pack Hunters

The Magna Steppe Grazer Guild exists in tight coevolution with the **Pack Hunter Guild**, notably:

- **Banded Dog Pack Hunter** � cursorial pursuit; harasses outer edges of Zebra and Sprint herds.  
- **Hexa-Cat Pack Hunter** � ambush and short-range burst attacks; favors breaking smaller groups or fringe individuals.  
- **Short Round Pack Hunter** � robust, endurance-oriented hunter and scavenger; specializes on injured or exhausted grazers and carcass exploitation.

Each grazer taxon presents a different **target profile**:

- Sprint: hardest to catch but most vulnerable if cornered or injured.  
- Buffalo: difficult to bring down, but high pay-off; short, intense conflicts.  
- Zebra: primary everyday prey, balancing detectability, abundance, and defense.  
- Hippo: primarily attacked at shorelines or low water; otherwise relatively secure.  
- Hog: targeted when foraging alone or in small groups.

### 6.2 Autotroph & Detritivore Linkages

- Main autotroph partners: SSRS-A �Grasses�, Brownflower Shrubs, and littoral reed-like autotrophs.  
- Dung and carcass material strongly supports:  
  - Saprotrophic guilds (soil �Slushbug� S/F-BLUs).  
  - Specialized scavenger and decomposer composites in the **Scavenger Guild**.

### 6.3 Keystone Effects

- Large-scale **grazing patterns** enforce grass dominance, suppress woody encroachment, and maintain open steppe.  
- **Trampling and wallows** (especially from Buffalo and Hippo Grazers) create small wetlands and disturbed patches.  
- **Hog Grazer digging** increases soil mixing and aeration, influencing germination patterns and water infiltration.

## 7. Evolutionary & Partifunct Perspective

### 7.1 Shared Partifunct Lineages

From the partifunct viewpoint, membership in the Magna Steppe Grazer Guild is largely defined by:

- Structural: `ST-BDY-GIRD-HEXAS/HEXAF`, `ST-LMB-SEGM-L3T3B` (and variants), `ST-LMB-TENT-*`, `ST-END-BLNT-*`.  
- Digestive: grazer-optimized `DG-GRD`, `DG-FRC`, `DG-ABS`, `DG-REP`, plus detoxifying `DG-SCB`.  
- Control & Sensory: `CN-MOT`, `CN-NAV`, `CN-TRT`, `CN-SOC` overlaid on `SN-OPT`, `SN-AUR`, `SN-OLC`, `SN-MAG`, `SN-RAD`.  
- Reproductive scaffolds: `RF-WMB`, `RF-SCF` lineages biased toward �large hexapedal plains grazer� configurations.

These lineages repeatedly �find� one another during womb assembly, forming **conservative, high-fitness combinations** in Zebra and Buffalo�style assemblies and more experimental configurations in Sprint, Hippo, and Hog forms.

### 7.2 Innovation vs Conservatism

- **Conservative taxa** (Zebra, Buffalo) display higher PSV and lower MDI, representing well-tested solutions favored over many assembly cycles.  
- **Innovative taxa** (Sprint, Hippo, Hog) push into higher MDI space, exploring cursorial, aquatic, and root-feeding niches; they show more frequent local variation and experimental morphs.

From a genode�s perspective, the Magna Steppe Grazer Guild is a **stable but ever-flexible coalition** of structural, digestive, and control partifunct lineages that have learned to cooperate effectively in an open-steppe, predator-rich landscape.

## 8. Research Notes & Open Questions

- Exact PSV/MDI values for Hippo and Hog Grazers remain provisional; more TPMs are needed from multiple regions.  
- Several intermediate forms (e.g. Dune Grazer) may justify full TPM treatment or recognition as distinct taxa peaks.  
- Long-distance migration routes and meta-herd dynamics are still poorly resolved; CN-NAV lineages likely encode richer spatial behavior than currently documented.  
- Comparative studies with non-Magna grazer guilds (if any) could clarify whether the **hexagirdle + six-limb grazer pattern** is globally convergent or locally dominant.
