## Physical Characteristics

### Size and Mass

TPIV is slightly smaller and less massive than Earth, with **93% of Earth's mass** and a mean diameter of approximately **11,650 km**. Despite the lower mass, the planet's composition produces a surface gravity of **0.91g**, making it remarkably comfortable for Earth-adapted organisms and equipment.

**Oblate Spheroid:**
- Equatorial bulge creates 52 km difference between equatorial and polar diameters
- Relatively modest flattening (similar to Earth's)
- Result of 31-hour rotation period

### Surface Gravity

At **0.91g**, TPIV's gravity is approximately 91% of Earth standard. This has several implications for life:

**Biological Effects:**
- Enables larger flying organisms (less energy needed for lift)
- Supports massive creatures in [[45-Flying-Forest|Flying Forest]] ecosystems
- Slightly reduces metabolic costs of locomotion
- May contribute to prevalence of hexapodal and octopodal body plans

**Human Operations:**
- Minimal adaptation required for personnel
- Slightly improved endurance and lifting capacity
- Negligible impact on equipment performance
- Comfortable long-term habitation conditions

### Planetary Composition

**Internal Structure:**
- Iron-nickel core (smaller than Earth's proportionally)
- Silicate mantle
- Thin crust (oceanic: ~6-8 km, continental: ~30-40 km)

**Magnetic Field:**
- Active magnetosphere
- Strength: ~0.4 Gauss at surface (Earth: 0.5 Gauss)
- Provides radiation protection
- Stable over geological timescales

**Surface Distribution:**
- **65% Ocean** (liquid water)
- **35% Land** (5 major continents)
- Extensive continental shelves
- Multiple large island chains

## Orbital Characteristics

### Rotation Period

**31 hours, 23 minutes, 18.25 seconds**

The slightly longer day than Earth creates unique circadian challenges for human personnel but appears well-suited to TPIV's native biology. Many organisms show activity patterns synchronized to the local day-night cycle.

**Day-Night Cycle Implications:**
- Longer daylight periods support autotroph productivity
- Extended nights create temperature drops
- Three-moon system creates complex lighting patterns at night
- Some organisms show **polyphasic activity** (multiple active periods per day)

### Orbital Period

**589 local days (773.6 Earth days)**

TPIV's orbit around its F8V primary is slightly longer than Earth's year, creating a roughly 2.1 Earth-year orbital period.

**Seasonal Implications:**
- Longer seasons than Earth (19° axial tilt)
- More time for seasonal adaptations
- Migration patterns can span multiple local months
- [[72c-Cyclical-Morphing|Cyclical morphing]] behaviors have time to fully express

### Axial Tilt

**19° inclination**

Slightly less than Earth's 23.5°, resulting in **moderate but distinct seasons**:

**Seasonal Effects:**
- Less extreme seasonal variation than Earth
- Polar regions experience milder winters
- Equatorial regions relatively stable year-round
- Mid-latitudes show clear four-season pattern
- Supports [[44-Magna-Steppe|Magna Steppe]] seasonal migration patterns

### Orbital Eccentricity

**1% (nearly circular)**

TPIV's nearly circular orbit ensures consistent solar radiation throughout the year, with minimal variation due to orbital distance.

**Result:**
- Seasons driven by axial tilt, not orbital distance
- Predictable climate patterns
- Stable long-term evolutionary pressures
- Consistent energy input for autotrophs

## Surface Features

### Continental Distribution

**Five Major Landmasses:**

1. **[[14a-Magna]]** - Largest continent, spans 70°N to 35°S
   - Equatorial position
   - Highest biodiversity
   - Site of [[94c-Camp-Aurora|Camp Aurora]] base
   
2. **[[14b-Dualis]]** - Two subcontinents connected by isthmus
   - Southern hemisphere
   - Tropical to Antarctic range
   - Critical "mixing zone" for partifuncts
   
3. **[[14c-Piedmont]]** - Smaller northern continent
   - Ancient eroded mountains
   - Connected to Magna
   
4. **[[14d-Apanon]]** - Circular island continent
   - Isolated evolution
   - Homeland of insect-communicating [[50-Distributed-Organisms-Overview|Distributed Organisms]]
   
5. **[[14e-Kolofluke]]** - Elongated island continent
   - Central mountain spine
   - Homeland of **radio-wave** Distributed Organisms
   - Contains unique [[46-Dark-Forest-Kolofluke|Dark Forest]] biome

**Continental Connections:**
- Magna-Dualis-Piedmont form connected supercluster
- Apanon and Kolofluke isolated (island continents)
- Isolation has driven unique evolutionary paths

### Ocean Basins

**Seven Named Oceans:**
- [[15a-PanTheta-Ocean|PanTheta]] - Largest, circumglobal
- [[15b-Long-Ocean|Long]] - Elongated basin
- [[15c-Dualis-Ocean|Dualis]] - Between Dualis subcontinents
- [[15d-Apaono-Ocean|Apaono]] - Surrounding Apanon
- [[15e-Pamagna-Ocean|Pamagna]] - Adjacent to Magna
- [[15f-Arctic-Ocean|Arctic]] - North polar
- [[15g-Antarctic-Ocean|Antarctic]] - South polar

**Ocean Characteristics:**
- Average depth: ~3,800 m (similar to Earth)
- Deepest trench: ~10,200 m
- Extensive continental shelves
- Major current systems driven by rotation and moon-tides

## Climate Overview

### Temperature Range

**Surface Temperature:** -50°C to +50°C

**Climate Zones:**
- **Polar:** Seasonal ice, winter-adapted life
- **Temperate:** Four distinct seasons
- **Tropical:** Year-round warmth, monsoon patterns
- **Arid:** Rain shadow zones, desert biomes

### Atmospheric Composition

See [[Atmosphere]] for detailed analysis.

**Brief Summary:**
- 60% Nitrogen
- 25% Oxygen (higher than Earth!)
- 5% Argon
- 1% Carbon Dioxide
- 9% Other gases (including higher methane, water vapor)

**Higher Oxygen Implications:**
- Supports larger organisms
- More efficient metabolism
- Enables [[45-Flying-Forest|Flying Forest]] ecosystem
- Contributes to brilliant colors in sky

### Weather Patterns

**Global Circulation:**
- Hadley cells similar to Earth
- Coriolis effect (31-hour rotation creates similar deflection patterns)
- Jet streams guide major storm systems
- Three-moon system creates complex tidal patterns

**Storm Systems:**
- Hurricanes/cyclones in tropical oceans
- Monsoon patterns in continental interiors
- Polar vortex systems in winter
- [[47-Central-Tidal-Lakes-Magna|Tidal lake systems]] create unique local weather

## Unique Planetary Features

### Three-Moon System

See [[Moons-and-Tides]] for complete analysis.

**The Three Moons:**
1. **[[13a-Tida-Primary-Moon|Tida]]** - Primary moon (1.3 Luna masses)
   - 24.6-day orbit
   - Drives strong tides
   
2. **[[13b-Lumna-Secondary-Moon|Lumna]]** - Secondary (0.5 Luna masses)
   - 47-day orbit
   - Highly reflective (bright nights)
   
3. **[[13c-Radita-Tertiary-Moon|Radita]]** - Tertiary (0.01 Luna masses)
   - 1.3-day orbit
   - High variable radiation source
   - Retrograde orbit

**Compound Effects:**
- **Super-tides** when moons align
- Frequent eclipses (multiple types)
- Complex tidal patterns → [[48-Intertidal-Zones|evolutionary hotspots]]
- Navigation cues for organisms
- Reproductive synchronization triggers

### Magnetic Field Characteristics

**Strength:** ~0.4 Gauss at surface

**Features:**
- Active dynamo in liquid outer core
- Stable polarity (no recent reversals detected)
- Van Allen-style radiation belts
- Protects surface from stellar radiation
- Enables [[13c-Radita-Tertiary-Moon|Radita]] radiation to be primary timing cue

**Biological Implications:**
- Some organisms use magnetoreception for navigation
- [[14e-Kolofluke|Kolofluke]] radio-wave organisms may use field for signal propagation
- Minimal radiation hazard for survey personnel

## Geological Activity

See [[16-Geologic-History]] for detailed analysis.

### Plate Tectonics

**Active System:**
- ~12 major tectonic plates
- Continental drift ongoing
- Subduction zones around continental margins
- Mid-ocean ridge system

**Activity Level:**
- Moderate (less active than Earth currently)
- [[14a-Magna|Magna]] sits on stable craton
- [[14e-Kolofluke|Kolofluke]] shows recent volcanic activity
- Seismic activity concentrated at plate boundaries

### Volcanism

**Current Activity:**
- Several active volcanoes on island chains
- Recent activity on Kolofluke (within 10,000 years)
- Contributes atmospheric gases
- Creates fertile soils in volcanic regions

### Erosion and Weathering

**High Oxygen Effects:**
- Accelerated chemical weathering
- Rapid oxidation of exposed minerals
- Contributes to soil fertility
- [[14c-Piedmont|Piedmont]] shows extensive ancient erosion
