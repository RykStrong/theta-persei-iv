# Quick Reference Card

**Essential TPIV Biology at a Glance**

Print-friendly single-page reference for the most critical concepts, terms, and distinctions.

---

## The Absolute Essentials

### What Makes TPIV Unique
1. **No DNA** → Genetic information stored in folded proteins (GFP)
2. **No cells** → Life based on Basic Life Units (BLUs), 100× larger
3. **No unified genome** → Each body part genetically distinct (partifuncts)
4. **No species** → Statistical groupings (taxa) with fluid boundaries
5. **No sexual reproduction** → Environmental assembly of genetic modules

### The Core Concept
> **Every organism is a modular assembly of genetically distinct parts (partifuncts) that cooperate to form a functional whole (composite organism).**

---

## Fundamental Terminology

### The Basics
| Term | Definition | Earth Analog |
|------|------------|--------------|
| **BLU** | Basic Life Unit - tissue-scale structure, 100× larger than cells | Cell (but not really) |
| **GFP** | Genomic Folded Protein - stores genetic information | DNA |
| **Genodule** | Bundle of GFP that expresses as a partifunct | Gene cluster |
| **Partifunct** | Genetically distinct biological module (limb, organ system, etc.) | NO EQUIVALENT* |
| **Composite Organism** | Assembly of multiple partifuncts forming functional creature | Individual organism |
| **eGFP** | Environmental GFP - genetic material floating in environment | NO EQUIVALENT |
| **Taxon** | Statistical grouping of similar composite assemblies | Species (but not really) |

*Most critical: **Partifuncts ≠ organs** (organs share DNA with body; partifuncts don't)

### Advanced Terms
| Term | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **PSV** | Partifunct Set Variance - % consistency of modules in taxon | 83% = very consistent |
| **MDI** | Morphological Divergence Index - variation in appearance | 4.2 = moderate variation |
| **TPM** | Taxon Partifunct Manifest - standard organism documentation | Like a field guide entry |
| **Partifzygote** | Developmental seed for one partifunct | Analogous to gamete |
| **Exovant** | Temporarily detached body part (Separated Behavior) | Throwjaw's jaw |
| **Partifvant** | One body of a Distributed Organism | DO "Horse" unit |
| **CEP** | Cognitive Exchange Packet - information unit for DOs | Neural signal packet |
| **Do-mite** | Insect-like carrier of CEPs between partifvants | Communication courier |

## The Three Levels of Biology

```
1. MOLECULAR LEVEL
   └─ GFP (genetic information)
        └─ Expresses as BLUs (tissue structures)

2. MODULE LEVEL  
   └─ Partifunct (functional unit with own GFP)
        └─ Can express in multiple contexts
        └─ Sheds eGFP into environment

3. ORGANISM LEVEL
   └─ Composite Organism (assembly of partifuncts)
        └─ Integrated but genetically diverse
        └─ Appears as single individual
```

## Critical Distinctions

### Partifunct vs. Organ vs. Zooid

| Feature | Earth Organ | Earth Zooid | TPIV Partifunct |
|---------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Genetic identity** | Shares 100% DNA | Shares 100% DNA | **Unique DNA** |
| **Independent viable?** | No | Limited | **Often yes** |
| **Reproduction** | No | Specialized zooids | **Yes** (via eGFP) |
| **Example** | Human heart | Portuguese Man-of-War gastrozooid | Zebra Grazer leg |

**Key:** Partifuncts are **genetically distinct individuals** cooperating as functional units.

### Taxon vs. Species

| Feature | Earth Species | TPIV Taxon |
|---------|---------------|------------|
| **Definition** | Interbreeding population | Statistical assemblage |
| **Boundaries** | Fixed (genetic) | Fluid (morphological) |
| **Offspring** | Resemble parents | Variable assemblies |
| **Classification** | Linnean hierarchy | Functional groupings |
| **Metric** | Genetic similarity | PSV + MDI |

**Key:** Taxa are **recurring patterns**, not genetic lineages.

### DO vs. SB vs. JB

| Type | Full Name | Viable Separately? | Communication | Example |
|------|-----------|-------------------|---------------|---------|
| **DO** | Distributed Organism | NO (permanent) | CEPs via do-mites | Horse Grazer |
| **SB** | Separated Behavior | NO (must rejoin) | Direct (attached) | Throwjaw |
| **JB** | Joined Behavior | YES (independent) | Chemical/passive | Stringbirds |

**Key:** DOs are **one organism** in many bodies; JB are **many organisms** temporarily joining.

## Reproduction Flowchart

### Womb-Based (Complex Organisms)
```
1. Adult organism exists in environment
   ↓
2. Breathes, eats, moves → collects eGFP
   ↓
3. Each partifunct:
   - Identifies compatible eGFP
   - Recombines with own GFP
   - Creates partifzygote
   ↓
4. Womb partifunct receives partifzygotes
   ↓
5. Womb evaluates & "polls" parent partifuncts
   ↓
6. Assembles compatible set
   ↓
7. Achieves metabolic integrity
   ↓
8. BIRTH → New composite organism
   (similar taxon, different assembly)
```

### Non-Womb (Simple Organisms)
```
1. Organism sheds GFP packets
   ↓
2. Packets self-organize in environment
   ↓
3. If viable → spontaneous organism
```

## Problem-Solving Quick Guide

### "How do I classify this organism?"
1. Document core partifunct set
2. Calculate PSV (consistency within sample)
3. Calculate MDI (morphological variation)
4. Compare to known taxa
5. Create TPM if novel

### "Is this a new species?"
**Wrong question.** Ask instead:
- "Does this represent a distinct taxon?"
- "What is its PSV/MDI?"
- "What partifunct assemblage does it use?"

### "How do these organisms reproduce?"
1. Check for womb partifunct → Womb-based
2. No womb? → Environmental assembly
3. Note: No sexes, no gametes, no fertilization

### "Why does this organism look so different from its parent?"
**Normal.** Different eGFP available + womb assembly variance = high morphological diversity within taxa.

## Key Questions This System Answers

**Q: Why no species?**
A: Horizontal gene transfer (eGFP) prevents fixed genetic lineages.

**Q: Why so variable within taxa?**
A: Each offspring is new assembly from available partifzygotes.

**Q: Why modularity evolved?**
A: Non-homochiral chemistry prevents direct genetic fusion.

**Q: How does womb know what to assemble?**
A: Polls parent partifuncts for compatibility; filters non-viable combinations.

**Q: What's the weirdest thing?**
A: Distributed Organisms using radio waves for cognition (Kolofluke).
