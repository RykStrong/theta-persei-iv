# Radita - Tertiary Moon

Radita (Tertiary Moon - "Chaos Generator")
    . Orbit: 55,000 km (decaying, retrograde)
    . Period: 1.2 local days (1.57 Earth days)
    . Inclination: 94° (Retrograde Polar)
    . Size: 480 km diameter (Irregular)
    . Mass: ~0.01 M☾
    . Notes: Emits high-frequency EM bursts with 6.4-hour pulse cadence. Non-tidally locked. Source of atmospheric ionization and navigational radiation timing. 
      Tidal contribution small (~0.5-3m) but chaotic due to incommensurate orbital  period and 3D geometry; creates unpredictable super-tide events.

---

## Physical Characteristics

**Size & Composition:**
- Tiny: ~35 km diameter (asteroid-sized)
- Mass: 0.01 M☽ (1% of Earth's moon)
- Dark surface (low albedo ~0.08)
- Rocky/metallic composition
- **Not tidally locked** - rotates independently

**Appearance from TPIV:**
- Barely visible to naked eye
- Appears as fast-moving dim point
- Completes orbit in 31 hours (faster than TPIV's rotation)
- Difficult to spot without tracking

---

## Orbital Characteristics

**Unusual Orbit:**
- Very close: ~85,000 km (compare: Tida at ~310,000 km)
- Fast: 1.3-day period (~31 hours)
- **Eccentric:** Noticeably elliptical
- **High-latitude inclined:** Not in equatorial plane
- **Retrograde:** Orbits opposite to TPIV's rotation

**Recent Capture:**
- Not tidally locked yet
- Suggests recent capture event (geologically)
- Unstable long-term orbit (will eventually crash or escape)
- May have influenced recent evolutionary events

---

## Radiation Emission

**Variable Radiation Source:**
- Emits detectable high-energy radiation
- Output varies as moon rotates (not tidally locked)
- Creates **predictable timing pulses**
- Possibly radioactive minerals or unusual composition

**Radiation Pattern:**
- Fluctuates on rotation period (~8 hours estimated)
- Combined with orbital motion creates complex pattern
- Detectable at TPIV surface (but not hazardous)
- Penetrates atmosphere

**Human Safety:**
- Radiation levels monitored
- **Not hazardous** to humans or equipment at surface
- Magnetic field provides shielding
- Atmospheric absorption reduces flux
- Standard precautions sufficient

## Biological Significance

### Radiation Detection

**Sensory Adaptations:**
- Many organisms have [[8Bd-SN-GSN-Gas-Sensing|radiation-sensing partifuncts]]
- Detect Radita's variable output
- Use for **navigation** and **timekeeping**
- Evolved specifically to track this signal

**Advantages over Visual Tracking:**
- Works in darkness
- Works underground/underwater
- Penetrates cloud cover
- More precise than visual observation

### Fine-Grained Timing

**1.3-Day Cycle:**
- Fastest lunar cycle
- Provides short-term temporal reference
- Combined with rotation creates complex pattern
- Enables precise synchronization

**Nested Timing Hierarchy:**
- **Short-term:** Radita (1.3 days)
- **Mid-term:** [[13a-Tida-Primary-Moon|Tida]] (24.6 days)
- **Long-term:** [[13b-Lumna-Secondary-Moon|Lumna]] (47 days)
- Organisms use appropriate scale for behavior

### Reproductive Triggers

**Possible Synchronization Cue:**
- Some organisms may time reproduction to Radita
- Radiation pulses could trigger [[72c-Cyclical-Morphing|cyclical morphing]]
- Short cycle allows frequent reproductive opportunities
- Combined with Tida/Lumna for complex timing

## Impact on Behavior

**Navigation:**
- [[84-Aerial|Aerial organisms]] may use radiation gradient
- Underground organisms sense Radita without seeing sky
- [[85-Aquatic|Aquatic organisms]] detect through water
- [[50-Distributed-Organisms-Overview|Distributed Organisms]] may use for coordination

**Activity Timing:**
- Some organisms show 1.3-day activity cycles
- Not tied to day/night (radiation always detectable)
- Enables temporal niche partitioning
- Independent of weather/cloud cover

**Migration Cues:**
- Fast cycle allows frequent "checkpoints"
- Can navigate by Radita even during overcast
- Possibly used for [[44-Magna-Steppe|long-distance migration]]

## Tidal Effects

**Negligible:**
- Too small and distant for significant tides
- Contributes <0.1 meter to tidal range
- Tidal effects dominated by [[13a-Tida-Primary-Moon|Tida]] and [[13b-Lumna-Secondary-Moon|Lumna]]

## Eclipse Events

**Radita Eclipses:**
- Too small to create noticeable solar eclipses
- Can be eclipsed by TPIV's shadow
- Eclipsed by Tida or Lumna occasionally
- Events brief and barely noticeable

**Scientific Interest:**
- Eclipses allow radiation measurements (source blocked)
- Tests organism response to signal loss
- Validates radiation detection hypothesis

## Capture History

**Recent Capture Evidence:**
- Not tidally locked (would be if ancient)
- Retrograde orbit (unusual for primary satellite)
- Eccentric, inclined orbit (unstable)
- Suggests capture within last ~10-100 million years

**Evolutionary Implications:**
- Organisms adapted to Radita relatively recently
- Rapid evolution of radiation-sensing partifuncts
- Demonstrates adaptability of [[Partifunct Core Definition|modular biology]]
- May explain some "recent" partifunct innovations

**Long-Term Fate:**
- Orbit will decay (tidal forces)
- Will eventually crash into TPIV or be ejected
- Timescale: millions of years
- Not a concern for current biosphere
