# Reading Order Guide

**For First-Time Visitors to the TPIV Wiki**

This guide provides a structured path through the xenobiological documentation of Theta Persei IV. The material is complex and often counterintuitive to Earth-trained biologists, so following this sequence will build understanding progressively.

---

## Quick Start (15 minutes)

**If you only have time for the essentials:**

1. [[Planet Overview]] - Where is TPIV? What's it like?
2. [[Biology Overview]] - The big picture of TPIV life
3. [[Partifunct Core Definition]] - The core concept that changes everything
4. [[TPM-Zebra-Grazer]] - See it all in action with one organism

**Key Takeaway:** Life on TPIV is modular. Every creature is an assembly of [[genetically distinct parts|Partifunct]] (a [[Composite Organisms]]), each with its own evolutionary history.

## Foundation Track (1-2 hours)

**For a solid grounding in TPIV biology:**

### Phase 1: The Planet (15 min)
Start with the physical environment to understand the constraints that shaped evolution.

1. **[[Planet-Overview]]** - Physical properties, atmosphere, gravity
   - *Why it matters:* Earth-like environment → convergent evolution, but with alien mechanisms
2. **[[Moons-and-Tides]]** - Three moon system creates unique pressures
   - *Why it matters:* Tidal zones are evolutionary hotspots; lunar cycles trigger reproduction
3. **[[Continents]]** - Five continents, varied biomes
   - *Quick focus:* [[Magna]] - Where most research has occurred

### Phase 2: Core Biology (30 min)
Build understanding from bottom up: molecules → modules → organisms.

4. **[[21-BLUs-Basic-Life-Units]]** - Not cells, but 100x larger tissue units
   - *Key concept:* BLUs don't contain full genomes
5. **[[GFP-and-Genodules]]** - Folded proteins store genetic information
   - *Key concept:* No DNA, no chromosomes as we know them
   - *Sub-topic:* [[Environmental-GFP]] - Genetic material floats in the environment
6. **[[Partifunct Core Definition]]** - The fundamental breakthrough
   - *Critical reading:* [[Partifunct-vs-Organ-vs-Zooid]] - What makes partifuncts unique
   - *Key concept:* Each body part is genetically distinct from the others
7. **[[Composite-Organisms]]** - How partifuncts assemble into "individuals"
   - *Key concept:* No two individuals are genetically identical

### Phase 3: Reproduction (20 min)
How does modular life reproduce?

8. **[[Reproduction-Assembly-Conception]]** - Not sex, but assembly
   - *Sub-topics:*
     - [[Womb-Enabled-Reproduction]] - Most complex organisms
     - [[Non-Womb-Reproduction]] - Simpler organisms ("plants")
9. **[[GFP-Carriers-and-Vectors]]** - Mites, pollen, and genetic packets
   - *Key concept:* The environment is a genetic marketplace
10. **[[3B-Womb-Partifunct-Systems]]** - How wombs "poll" and assemble
    - *Key concept:* Quality control prevents non-viable combinations

### Phase 4: Classification (15 min)
How do we make sense of organisms without species?

11. **[[31-Taxa-and-Metrics-PSV-MDI]]** - Statistical approach to taxonomy
    - *Key metrics:* PSV (consistency) and MDI (variation)
12. **[[TPM-Template]]** - Standard documentation format
13. **[[TPM-Zebra-Grazer]]** - Complete example of a documented organism
    - *Why this one:* Most thoroughly studied, represents "typical" pattern

## Common Pitfalls

### ❌ Don't Start Here:
- **Dispatches** - Primary sources are dense without context
- **Partifunct Catalog** - Too granular without big picture
- **Distributed Organisms** - Too advanced without foundation
- **Microbiosphere** - Interesting but not essential first

### ⚠️ Confusion Points:
Watch out for these common misunderstandings:

1. **"Partifuncts are like organs"** 
   - NO: Organs share DNA with whole body; partifuncts don't
   - Read: [[Partifunct-vs-Organ-vs-Zooid]]

2. **"Taxa are like species"**
   - NO: Taxa are statistical clusters, not genetic lineages
   - Read: [[31-Taxa-and-Metrics-PSV-MDI]]

3. **"Distributed Organisms are like ant colonies"**
   - CLOSER: But ant colonies have many genetic individuals; DOs are one individual in many bodies
   - Read: [[50a-DO-vs-SB-vs-JB-Comparison]]

4. **"There are plants and animals"**
   - NO: All life exists on a continuum
   - Read: [[41-Trophic-Roles-No-Plant-Animal]]

5. **"Offspring look like parents"**
   - SOMEWHAT: They share partifunct types but not exact configurations
   - Read: [[24b-Non-Homogeneity]]

## Tips for Understanding

### 1. Let Go of Earth Assumptions
TPIV biology works. It's just different. Don't force it into Earth categories.

### 2. Build Mental Models Progressively
- **Week 1:** Think "Lego blocks with DNA"
- **Week 2:** Think "each Lego block has its own DNA"
- **Week 3:** Think "Lego blocks float in environment; organisms grab compatible ones"
- **Week 4:** Think "what if the Lego blocks could communicate across multiple structures?"

### 3. Use the Zebra Grazer as Your Anchor
Whenever confused, return to [[TPM-Zebra-Grazer]]. It's the most thoroughly documented organism and represents "typical" patterns.

### 4. Follow the Dispatches for Discovery Experience
Reading [[97-Dispatches]] in order lets you experience the confusion and breakthroughs the survey team went through.

### 5. Visual Thinking Helps
- BLUs = large tissue chunks (imagine grapes, not cells)
- Partifuncts = distinct colored sections of a Lego model
- Composite organisms = the complete Lego model
- eGFP = floating Lego blocks in the air
- Womb = assembly station that picks compatible blocks
