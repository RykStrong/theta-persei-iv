## Selfish Gene, TPIV style

From a genode/partifunct perspective, the universe looks like this:

**1.**      **Genes are encoded in GFP**

**o**        **GFP is gathered into bundles**

**o**        **3D conformation stores genetic data**

2.      **GFP bundles → Genodes**

o        A **genode** (genodule) is a set of GFP bundles that, taken together, reliably express a particular functional unit.

o        Think: “the code for _this_ partifunct lineage” – a limb segment, a digestive loop, a sensory stalk, a cognitive module, etc.

3.      **Genodes → Partifuncts**

o        A **partifunct** is the embodied, BLU-scale expression of one genode set in a given context.

o        It’s a **self-contained biological module** with:

§         Its own BLU architecture.

§         Its own metabolic loop.

§         Its own GFP management (MGFP chains, etc.).

o        It can exist:

§         As a free-standing organism (grass-phase, mite-phase, BLU-aggregate, etc.), or

§         As a “component organ” inside a composite creature.

4.      **Partifunct Coalitions → Composite Organisms**

o        Composite “animals” and “plants” are **coalitions of partifuncts** that have evolved to cooperate under a common hormonal, mechanical, and cognitive regime.

o        No shared genome. Only **shared frameworks** and compatibility signaling.

5.      **Assemblies of Composites → Guilds / Taxa**

o        Guilds (grazer, pack hunter, aerial hunter, etc.) and their taxa (Zebra Grazer, Banded Dog Pack Hunter, etc.) are **persistent patterns** of which partifuncts tend to work together under which environmental regimes.

From this POV, a “Zebra Grazer” is not a unitary organism. It’s:

“The recurring set of contracts under which a particular cluster of genode lineages get themselves embodied in a mid-sized, hexapodal, ground-grazing composite on Magna.”

## The Life-Cycle of a Genode

Forget individuals; imagine you are a **genode lineage**—say, **ST-LMB-LIGN1**, the classic limb/bone segment.

Your life cycle looks roughly like this:

1.      **Encoding Phase – GFP Bundles**

o        Your “body” is a pattern in **GFP bundles**, possibly with moiety/sex-like structure for controlled recombination.

o        You’re carried inside:

§         Existing partifuncts that express you (limbs, stalks, etc.).

§         Environmental vectors (mites, pollen analogues, BLU packets, “seeds”).

2.      **Dispersal Phase – eGFP**

o        Your GFP fragments are continually shed as **eGFP** gametes

§         You continually produce gametes and shed the eGFP in feces, pollen-like dust, BLU sloughing, breeding swarms, seeds, etc.

o        You compete with rival variants for:

§         Physical transport.

§         Stability in air/water/soil.

§         Recognition by womb and partifunct filters.

3.      **Recruitment Phase – Selection by Wombs / Partifuncts**

o        You are **pulled out of the environment** when:

§         A partifunct inside a Composite Organism selects you for recombination and you form a partifzygote

§         A womb partifunct is assembling a new composite and needs your function class

o        At this moment your fate is decided:

§         Included in a viable partifunct → you get expressed again.

§         Passed over or filtered out → you decay, or your line continues only via other copies.

4.      **Expression Phase – Partifunct Lifetime**

o        As a limb partifunct, you now:

§         Grow BLUs into rigid segments.

§         Link to joint partifuncts and vascular interfaces.

§         Contribute to locomotion, support, or other structural functions.

§         Shed your eGFP gametes into the environment.

o        You help your **host composite**:

§         Move efficiently.

§         Survive long enough to reproduce (assemble new composites).

§         Access environments where your lineages will be harvested again.

5.      **Feedback Phase – Fitness Accounting**  
Over many assemblies and many composites, your lineage is “judged” by:

o        **Breadth of viable contexts:**  
Can you be used in grazers, pack hunters, semi-aquatics, flying forest forms, etc.?

o        **Reliability:**  
Composites built with you fail less often than those built with competitors.

o        **Integration cost:**  
How much metabolic overhead do you impose? How complex are your interface demands?

Genodes do not “think,” but selection produces behavior _as if_ each lineage were trying to:

“Maximize the number and stability of contexts in which it is indispensable.”
