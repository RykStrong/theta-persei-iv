# Skeletal Systems

**Primary Materials:** Lignin-based (75%) or calcium-based (25%)  
**Architecture:** Segmented bones with separate joint structures  
**Key Feature:** Bones tend to be made of lignin or similar proteins  
**Central Structure:** Hexagirdle or octagirdle ring (most common)

---

## Skeletal Materials

### Two Primary Types

**Lignin-Based Bones (75%):**
Bones tend to be made of Lignin or similar proteins rather than calcium. The Lignin based bones are 75% of the total.

**Advantages:**
- Lighter than calcium
- Flexible when thin
- Very strong when thick
- Easy to repair/grow
- Less brittle than calcium

**Calcium-Based Bones (25%):**
- Similar to Earth bone
- Heavier but more rigid
- Better compressive strength
- Used in specialized structures
- Some organisms use both types

**Mixed Systems:**
- Some organisms use lignin for limbs, calcium for girdle
- Hybrid structures in large organisms
- Selection based on functional demands
- [[37a-Lignin-vs-Calcium-Bones|Detailed comparison]]

## Bone Structure

### Segmented Architecture

**[[88a-ST-LMB-Lignin-Limb-Segment|Limb Segments]]:**
- Bones composed of distinct segments
- Each segment may be separate partifunct component
- Rigid structural units
- Connected by joints

**Internal Architecture:**
- Dense outer layer (cortical bone analog)
- Porous inner structure (trabecular bone analog)
- [[LU-Biology|Genode clusters]] maintain bone tissue
- Vascular channels for nutrient delivery

---

## Joint Architecture

### Swollen Joints

**Distinctive Feature:**
Creatures tend to have joints that are separate segments from the limbs, giving a 'swollen joint' appearance to many limbed organisms.

**Structure:**
- [[37b-Joint-Architecture|Joints as distinct structures]]
- Often larger diameter than bone segments
- Ball-and-socket, hinge, or sliding configurations
- Muscular and connective tissue concentrated here

**Types:**
- **Ball-and-socket:** Wide range of motion (hip, shoulder analogs)
- **Hinge:** Single plane movement (knee, elbow analogs)
- **Sliding:** Limited gliding motion
- **Rotational:** Twisting capability

## Central Skeletal Structure

### Hexagirdle and Octagirdle

**[[Hexagirdle-Octagirdle|Ring-Shaped Body Core]]:**
On TPIV, the basic pattern of a hexapod, with a ring shaped bony hip/shoulder girdle with the central nervous trunk, and the brain and other critical organs protected in a body cavity in the center between or on top of the limbs has come to be very common.  The 'girdles' come in two main variations: flexible and rigid.  The girdle can be repeated to form a longer body with a 'chain link' skeleton.

**Hexagirdle (Most Common):**
- Ring-shaped bone structure
- Six limb attachment points
- [[33-Brain-Partifuncts|Brain]] and organs protected in center
- Central nerve loop runs through girdle
- Can be jointed or solid

**Octagirdle (Less Common):**
- Eight limb attachment points
- Larger body capacity
- Often in bigger organisms
- More complex musculature

**Function:**
- Protects vital organs
- Distributes limb forces
- Anchors muscles
- Houses central nervous system

## Limb Attachment

### Partifunct Integration

**Example Integration:**
The legs ST-LMB partifunct's bone ends in a ball which fits into a socket on the hexagirdle, the muscles of the leg partifunct have a tendon that attaches to the girdle, and muscles of the girdle attach via tendon to the leg bone.

**Physical Connection:**
- Ball-and-socket joints at limb-girdle interface
- Tendons span partifunct boundaries
- Muscles of limb attach to girdle
- Muscles of girdle attach to limb
- Cannot separate alive, but boundaries visible in dissection

## Research Observations

### Comparative Studies

**Across Taxa:**
- [[TPM-Zebra-Grazer|Zebra Grazers]]: Lignin limbs, mixed girdle (83% lignin, 17% calcium)
- Large predators: Heavier calcium content for strength
- Small organisms: Pure lignin (weight savings)
- Aerial: Hollow lignin bones (maximum lightness)

**Material Analysis:**
- Lignin bones 40% lighter than calcium equivalent
- Lignin more flexible (less brittle)
- Calcium better for compression (girdle, spine analogs)
- Both materials successful (complementary niches)
