# Taxa and Metrics: PSV & MDI

**Taxa Definition:** Statistical assemblage—recurring configuration of partifuncts that produces recognizable body plan or ecological role  
**Not Species:** No genetic unity, fuzzy boundaries, variable membership  
**Quantified By:** PSV (consistency) and MDI (variation)  
**Key Insight:** Repeatable phenotype, not genetic lineage

---

## What is a Taxon?

### Statistical Assemblage

A **taxon** on TPIV refers to a **statistical assemblage**: a common configuration of partifuncts that occurs frequently enough to produce a recognizable body plan or ecological role.

It is not a genetic species. It is a **repeatable phenotype**—a recurring composite structure that emerges from shared environmental and developmental conditions.

**No Central Genome:**
There is no single partifunct or genome that defines the composite forms of higher animals. Their form is determined by the selection pressures of the environment, acting on the partifuncts that make up their parts.

---

## Why Not Species?

### The Continuum of Life

What we call species do not exist on TPIV. What we have is a continuum of life forms that reach across a range of environments.

**Example:**
With grazing animals, on Earth there are deer, sheep, cattle, and camels (among others), while on TPIV there are a range of creatures from the size of a large dog through the size of a large bison that share different manifestations of the same partifuncts.

Looking at a prairie, one could see small grazers like antelope, and large ones like elephants, but also creatures of every step and stage between the two forms.

---

## The Two Metrics

### PSV: Partifunct Set Variance

**Definition:** How consistent is the set of modules across individuals?

PSV describes how consistently a given set of partifuncts occurs within the observed composite group.

**Presented as:** A percentage indicating how many individual organisms share the group's common core of partifuncts within ±1 standard deviation.

**Example Statement:**
*"PSV of Savannah Bison Grazer (LGW-Hx-2M) = 84.6% within 1σ."*

**Meaning:** 84.6% of observed individuals contain the standard partifunct set within one standard deviation of variance from the average.

---

### MDI: Morphological Divergence Index

**Definition:** How different do those individuals look, despite sharing modules?

MDI is expressed numerically to indicate how much morphological variation exists in the actual expression (physical form) of a given set of partifuncts within the composite group.

**Scale:** 
- **Lower MDI (<2):** Very uniform shape
- **Higher values (>5):** Significant morphological diversity

**Example Statement:**
*"MDI of Savannah Bison Grazer = 3.2."*

**Meaning:** Moderate variation; herd members display noticeable but generally minor morphological differences (e.g., some have extra sensory organs, variable limb count, etc.).

---

## Taxon Examples

### High PSV, Moderate MDI

**[[TPM-Zebra-Grazer|Zebra Grazer]]:**
- PSV: 83.2% (±1σ)
- MDI: 4.6
- Consistent partifunct set
- Look somewhat alike
- Share many partifuncts

**Interpretation:** Stable taxon with recognizable form but noticeable individual variation.

---

### Low PSV, High MDI

**[[83-LBB-Little-Brown-Birds|LBB Feeders]]:**
- PSV: 23%
- MDI: 8.2
- Near-chaotic diversity
- Built from common parts
- Highly variable appearance

**Interpretation:** Diffuse assemblage, catch-all category with extreme variation.

## Visualizing Taxa

### Partifunct Expression Graph (PEG)

To visualize these statistics, xenobiologists plot the observed organisms as multidimensional points on a **Partifunct Expression Graph (PEG)**:

- Each dimension of PEG corresponds to a specific measurable trait or the quantitative presence/expression level of certain partifuncts
- The resulting point cloud illustrates visually the shape of a taxon:
  - **Tight Cluster**: Low MDI, high PSV
  - **Diffuse Cloud**: High MDI, lower PSV

Scientists then quantify this shape numerically (PSV%, MDI value), allowing objective comparison between groups.

## Example Research Notes

### Standard Notation

**PEG Note:**
*"Composite 'Zebra Grazer' forms tight clusters (PSV=87%) but remains morphologically diverse (MDI=4.8), particularly in limb number and joint types."*

**Field Observation:**
*"The population sampled near the Olan River crossing exhibited a PSV of 92% (indicating high partifunct consistency) but had an elevated MDI of 5.4. The river-crossing behavior appears to encourage wider morphological expression—approximately 35% more variable than average populations, likely reflecting adaptive pressures."*

---

### Succinct Field Reports

Field researchers typically give succinct statements summarizing these statistical descriptors, like:

*"Observed group identified as 'River Guardian' variant, PSV 76%, MDI 6.1—indicating significant morphological and functional variability."*

These statistics convey a clear snapshot of the composite organisms' consistency and variability, efficiently substituting traditional biological definitions of "species."

## The Four-Quadrant Model

### PSV × MDI Patterns

| | **Low MDI** | **High MDI** |
|---|---|---|
| **High PSV** | Stable, uniform taxon<br>(Standard configuration) | Stable but variable taxon<br>(Same parts, different looks) |
| **Low PSV** | Uniform appearance, variable genetics<br>(Convergent, rare) | Chaotic diversity<br>(Catch-all category) |

## Offspring Variability

### Why Herds Look Different

What is possible to assemble and exactly how it is assembled can vary greatly, so a herd of grazers (for example) do not appear uniform in the way a herd of antelope do.

**Example - Grazer Herd:**
- Some grazers will have more legs, some fewer
- Some will have two mouths
- Some will have additional genomic forms as well as the basic three mentioned above
- Some individuals will have additional appendages for perception, defense, signaling, etc.

**The womb does not produce a copy of the parent** because there is no genome of the parent. The [[3B-Womb-Partifunct-Systems|womb]] assembles a 'near copy' based on [[Environmental-GFP|eGFP]] availability and conditions and this allows for a huge range of taxon variability.

## What Affects PSV

### Environmental Stability

**Stable Environments:**
- High PSV (proven combinations replicate)
- [[Womb-Enabled-Reproduction|Wombs]] favor conservative assembly
- Selection reinforces successful pattern

**Variable Environments:**
- Lower PSV (multiple strategies viable)
- Wombs experiment more
- Adaptive pressure for flexibility

---

### Population History

**Established Taxa:**
- Long history = stable partifunct sets
- High PSV common
- Self-reinforcing pattern

**Emerging Taxa:**
- New assemblages = variable sets
- Low PSV initially
- May stabilize or fragment

---

## What Affects MDI

### Expression Plasticity

**[[Multi-Expression-Potential|Multi-Expression]] Partifuncts:**
- Same genodule → many forms
- [[MGFP-Transcription-Pathway|MGFP-G]] interprets womb signals differently
- Environmental variation increases MDI

---

### Environmental Heterogeneity

**Variable Conditions:**
- Different temperatures → different bone density
- Different food availability → different sizes
- Different predator types → different defensive structures
- Womb adapts each offspring to current conditions

---

### Optional Partifuncts

**Additional Modules:**
- Some individuals have extra sensory organs
- Some have defensive structures
- Some have enhanced mobility features
- Increases visible variation without changing core set

---

## Naming Conventions

### Functional Classification

We use functional and visual descriptors to make up the common name of the composite organism.

**Functional names use:**
- Energy source
- Mode of locomotion (or anchoring)
- Bodily function or structure
- Size of the life form

**Convention:** Two words—one descriptive, one functional. No absolute rules.

**Examples:**
- "Zebra Grazer": Pattern + ecological role
- "Drophunter Blimp": Behavior + form
- "Crimson Strider": Color + locomotion

## Documentation Format

### [[TPM-Template|Taxon Partifunct Manifest (TPM)]]

Standard format for documenting taxa includes:

**Header:**
- Common name
- Designation code
- Ecological classification
- Location
- PSV and MDI values

**Core Partifunct Set:**
- Tracked partifuncts representing ~90% of body mass
- Functional roles
- Subtypes

**Statistical Summary:**
- PSV analysis
- MDI analysis
- Sample size (n)

## Research Applications

### Field Identification

**Process:**
1. Visual assessment
2. Identify apparent partifunct set
3. Measure morphological variation
4. Assign to closest known taxon
5. Note: May be intermediate/novel form

---

### Ecological Monitoring

**Track Changes:**
- PSV rising = population stabilizing
- PSV falling = environmental change/adaptation
- MDI rising = increased selective pressure for variation
- MDI falling = selection narrowing optimal form

---

### Conservation

**Different Priorities:**
- Earth: Preserve species (genetic lineages)
- TPIV: Preserve partifunct diversity (genetic toolkit)
- Rare partifuncts more important than rare taxa
- Ecosystem-wide genetic diversity critical

## Comparison to Earth

| **Feature** | **Earth Species** | **TPIV Taxa** |
|-------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Definition** | Genetic/reproductive | Statistical/phenotypic |
| **Boundaries** | Discrete | Fuzzy |
| **Genetic Unity** | Yes (shared genome) | No (unique individuals) |
| **Inheritance** | Vertical (parents) | Horizontal (environment) |
| **Stability** | High (barring mutation) | Moderate (selection-dependent) |
| **Intermediates** | Rare | Common |
| **Quantification** | Binomial nomenclature | PSV + MDI metrics |
