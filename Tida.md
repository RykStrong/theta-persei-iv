# Tida - Primary Moon

- Orbit: ~95,000 km
- Period: 0.667 local days (20.9 Earth hours)
- Orbital Resonance: 3:2 with planetary rotation
- Size: 0.28 R☾ (~1,950 km diameter)
- Mass: 1.3 M☾
- Notes: Prograde, tidally locked. Completes ~1.5 orbits per local day, creating semi-diurnal tidal pattern with high tide every ~21 hours. Rapid apparent motion across sky; full phase cycle every 16 hours. Main driver of baseline oceanic tides (10-15m range).

---

## Physical Characteristics

**Size & Composition:**
- 30% more massive than Earth's moon
- Radius: ~1,950 km
- Rocky body with iron core
- Ancient cratered surface, no atmosphere
- Tidally locked (same face always visible)

**Appearance from TPIV:**
- Slightly larger than Luna appears from Earth
- Gray with distinct dark maria
- Visible patterns used for navigation
- Predictable 24.6-day phase cycle

## Tidal Effects

### Standard Tides
- **Typical range:** 8-12 meters (twice daily)
- Creates vast [[48-Intertidal-Zones|intertidal zones]]
- High tide every ~15.7 hours

### Super-Tides (with Lumna)
- When aligned with [[13b-Lumna-Secondary-Moon|Lumna]]: **15+ meter range**
- Occurs every ~47 days
- Submerges extensive coastal areas
- Triggers mass spawning events

### Neap Tides
- When opposed to Lumna: 5-8 meter range
- Calmer periods for coastal organisms

## Geological Impact

**Tidal Heating:**
- Interior flexing generates heat
- Maintains TPIV's geological activity
- Sustains plate tectonics and volcanism
- Powers magnetic field

**Long-Term Evolution:**
- Gradually receding from TPIV
- TPIV's rotation slowing
- Stable for billions of years

## Biological Significance

### Evolutionary Driver
- [[48-Intertidal-Zones|Tidal zones]] experience extreme daily conditions
- Submersion/exposure cycles favor [[Partifunct Core Definition|modular biology]]
- Rapid environmental change accelerates [[Environmental-GFP|eGFP]] exchange
- Life may have originated in tidal pools

### Reproductive Timing
- Many organisms synchronize reproduction to Tida's phases
- Mass spawning during specific tidal events
- [[GFP-Carriers-and-Vectors|eGFP carriers]] released at optimal tides
- Tidal mixing increases genetic diversity

### Navigation & Behavior
- Primary timing reference (24.6-day cycle)
- Migratory species track phases
- Behavioral shifts triggered by phase changes
- Full moon increases nocturnal activity

## Eclipse Events

**Solar Eclipses:**
- Total eclipses possible (Tida larger than stellar disk)
- 2-4 times per TPIV year
- Duration up to 8 minutes
- Triggers behavioral responses in some organisms

**Lunar Eclipses:**
- Tida turns red-orange in TPIV's shadow
- Several times per year
- Visible across entire night side
