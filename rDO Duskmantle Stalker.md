# rDO-Duskmantle Stalker

**Classification:** Ambush Predator (Distributed, Radio-Based)  
**Biome:** [Kolofluke](Continents.md) [Dark Forest](Dark%20Forest%20Kolofluke.md) regions  
**PSV:** 71% (±1σ)  
**MDI:** 7.1  
**Communication:** Radio wave emissions (electromagnetic)

---

## Overview

### Radio-Based Predator DO

**rDO-Duskmantle Stalker** is the apex predator of [Kolofluke's](Continents.md) [Dark Forest](Dark%20Forest%20Kolofluke.md) biome, utilizing electromagnetic communication to coordinate hunting in near-total darkness.

**Key Features:**
- Multiple specialized [partivant](Partivant%20Definition.md) types adapted for darkness
- Radio wave communication (unique to Kolofluke)
- Ambush hunting strategy coordinated across wide areas
- Extreme low-light adaptations

**Observational Note (Day 40):**
*"In the deeply shadowed hills of Kolofluke there seems to be a set of taxa that are using radio communications to facilitate distributed organism neurological tasks."*

## Why Radio Communication?

### Dark Forest Adaptation

**Environmental Pressures:**
- [Dark Forest](Dark%20Forest%20Kolofluke.md) biome: Sunless valleys created by [Flying Forest](Flying%20Forest.md) blocking sunlight
- [Do-mites](CEP%20and%20Do-Mites.md) need light for navigation (cannot function in darkness)
- Dense vegetation blocks chemical signals
- Constant low-light conditions favor electromagnetic signals

**Radio Advantages:**
- Works in complete darkness
- Penetrates vegetation and terrain
- Longer range (10+ km observed)
- Faster signal transmission than chemical
- Weather-independent

**Energy Cost:**
- Requires specialized [electrogenesis partifuncts](Trophic%20Roles%20No%20Plant%20Animal.md)
- Higher metabolic demand than do-mites
- Trade-off accepted for superior coordination in darkness

## Partivant Types

### Specialized for Darkness

**Scout Nodes:**

**Structure:**
- Lean, stealthy build
- Enhanced [low-light vision](Sensory%20Systems.md) (massive pupils)
- Echolocation capability (some lineages)
- Long legs for silent movement
- Minimal digestive system

**Function:**
- Locate prey in near-darkness
- Track movement via sound and infrared
- Relay position data via radio
- Monitor Dark Forest pathways

**Sensory Adaptations:**
- Extreme low-light vision (rod-heavy retinas)
- Infrared sensitivity (detect body heat)
- Enhanced hearing (prey movement detection)
- Chemical sensors (pheromone tracking in still air)

**Behavior:**
- Move silently through undergrowth
- Utilize [Lumna's](Lumna.md) faint light
- Radio broadcast constant position updates
- Spread across wide area (1-3 km apart)

---

### Attack Nodes

**Structure:**
- Powerful muscular build
- Silent movement adaptations (padded feet)
- Enhanced [MOTO brain](Nervous%20Systems.md) (precise coordination)
- Strong jaws and claws
- Minimal vision (rely on radio positioning)

**Function:**
- Execute coordinated ambush
- Kill large prey in darkness
- Coordinate via radio signals exclusively
- Initial feeding on high-energy organs

**Behavior:**
- Receive position data from scouts (radio)
- Approach silently from multiple directions
- Radio-synchronized simultaneous strike
- Attack in complete darkness (no need for visual confirmation)

**Unique Capability:**
Can coordinate attacks on prey they cannot see—scout nodes provide positioning, attack nodes trust radio data completely.

---

### Feeding Nodes

**Structure:**
- Large body cavity
- Extensive digestive system
- Strong jaws (bone-crushing)
- Slow movement
- Storage capacity for extended periods

**Function:**
- Process kills made by attack nodes
- Extract maximum nutrition from carcass
- Store energy for lean periods
- Distribute nutrients minimally (attack nodes mostly self-sufficient)

**Behavior:**
- Follow radio signals to kill site
- Thorough carcass processing over days
- Remain at site while scouts continue hunting
- Return to den periodically

---

### Sensory Nodes

**Structure:**
- Massive ear structures (parabolic collectors)
- Minimal eyes (nearly blind)
- Echolocation organs (active sound production)
- Chemical sensors
- Long neck for elevated listening

**Function:**
- Long-range acoustic detection
- Echolocation mapping of terrain
- Monitor Dark Forest for threats
- Coordinate with scout nodes

**Specialization:**
- Some emit ultrasonic pulses (echolocation)
- Some passive listeners (prey sounds, environmental)
- Some infrared specialists (heat detection)
- [Sensory integration](Distributed%20Organisms%20Overview.md) distributed across partivants

---

### Cognitive Nodes

**Structure:**
- Multiple [brain partifuncts](Nervous%20Systems.md) (MEMO, STRA, NAVI, SENS)
- Well-protected in den sites
- Moderate size
- Enhanced radio transmission/reception capability

**Function:**
- [Distributed cognition](Distributed%20Organisms%20Overview.md) coordination
- Strategic planning (ambush site selection)
- Memory storage (Dark Forest geography, successful ambush points)
- Decision-making integration across all partivants
- Radio signal routing (communication hub)

**Intelligence:**
- Among most cognitively complex DOs
- Three-dimensional terrain mapping (stored in NAVI)
- Predictive modeling of prey movement
- Adaptation to prey defensive behaviors

---

### Radio Relay Nodes (Unique to rDOs)

**Structure:**
- Minimal body (mostly electrogenesis organs)
- Stationary or very slow-moving
- Large radio transmission/reception organs
- Minimal other systems

**Function:**
- Extend radio range beyond partivant separation limits
- Relay signals between distant partivants
- Amplify weak signals
- Enable territory size >10 km

**Placement:**
- Positioned on high ground or in caves (signal propagation)
- Create radio network topology
- 3-5 relay nodes per rDO typical
- Most vulnerable partivants (attacked by competitors)

## Radio Communication System

### Electromagnetic Integration

**Transmission:**
- Specialized [electrogenesis partifuncts](Trophic%20Roles%20No%20Plant%20Animal.md) generate radio waves
- Frequency range: ~10-100 MHz (estimated)
- Simultaneous amplitude and frequency modulation encodes information
- Multiple channels (simultaneous signals)

**Reception:**
- Dedicated receptor organs (electromagnetic-sensitive tissues)
- Neural processing of radio patterns
- Pattern recognition (signal → meaning)
- Integration with [SENS brain](Nervous%20Systems.md)

**Signal Types:**
- **Position data:** Scout locations, prey locations (GPS-like coordinates)
- **Threat alerts:** Predator warnings, danger signals
- **Coordination:** Attack timing, movement vectors (precise)
- **Status updates:** Partivant health, hunger levels
- **Terrain data:** Echolocation results shared via radio
- **Memory queries:** "What ambush points worked before?"

**Advantages Over Chemical:**
- No light needed (critical in Dark Forest)
- Instant transmission (vs. do-mite flight time)
- Penetrates dense vegetation
- Longer range (10-15 km vs. 1 km max for do-mites)

## Hunting Strategy

### Darkness Ambush

**Phase 1 - Detection:**
- Scout partivants patrol Dark Forest (using echolocation, infrared)
- Detect prey via sound, heat signature, chemical traces
- Radio position to cognitive nodes (instant)
- Cognitive nodes evaluate opportunity

**Phase 2 - Positioning:**
- Attack nodes receive precise movement instructions (radio)
- Navigate in complete darkness using radio guidance
- Approach from multiple vectors (coordinated via radio pulses)
- Silent movement (padded feet, careful placement)
- No visual contact needed—trust radio positioning

**Phase 3 - Ambush:**
- Cognitive node broadcasts "attack" signal (radio burst)
- All attack nodes strike simultaneously
- Prey cannot see attackers in darkness
- Scouts block escape routes (radio-guided positioning)
- Kill executed in seconds

**Phase 4 - Processing:**
- Attack nodes feed immediately (no waiting)
- Radio signal guides feeding nodes to location
- Cognitive nodes store success data
- Scouts resume patrolling

**Unique Aspect:**
Entire hunt can occur in complete darkness—prey never sees predators, predators never see prey.

## Territory and Range

### Wide-Area Radio Coordination

**Territory Size:**
- 20-50 km² typical
- Much larger than chemical DOs
- Enabled by long-range radio and relay nodes
- Defended against other rDO-Duskmantle packs

**Partivant Distribution:**
- Scout partivants: Widely dispersed (2-5 km apart)
- Attack partivants: Clustered near common prey paths
- Feeding partivants: Mobile
- Cognitive partivants: Central den sites
- Relay nodes: Fixed positions on high ground
- Maximum observed separation: 15 km (scout to cognitive node)

**[Radio Interference Conflicts](DO-Mite%20conflict.md):**
- Neighboring packs on overlapping frequencies
- Territorial battles fought via signal jamming
- Winner occupies frequency, loser forced to shift
- Physical combat rare (radio dominance sufficient)

## Reproduction

### Complex Assembly Challenge

**Reproductive Partivants:**
Created as needed during denning season.

**Challenge:**
- Must create multiple specialized partivant types
- Radio communication must function from birth
- Relay nodes must be positioned correctly
- Higher failure rate than chemical DOs (extremely complex coordination)
- [Womb](Womb%20Partifunct%20Systems.md) in reproductive partivant highly sophisticated

**Birth:**
- Produces 4-8 offspring partivants initially
- Core set: 1 cognitive, 2 scouts, 2 attack, 1 feeding, 1-2 relay
- Radio coordination tested immediately
- Non-viable sets die within hours (cannot coordinate)

**Development:**
- Additional partivants added over time
- Collect [eGFP](Environmental%20GFP.md) from prey and environment
- Full adult complement: 18-30 partivants (higher than chemical DOs due to relays)
- Maturation: 2-3 local years

## Ecological Role

### Dark Forest Apex Predator

**Prey:**
- Large herbivores (adapted to darkness)
- Medium-sized browsers
- Other predators (opportunistic)
- Even other DOs (if opportunity arises)

**Ecosystem Impact:**
- Regulates herbivore populations in Dark Forest
- Prevents overgrazing of sparse understory vegetation
- [Selection pressure](Natural%20Selection%20on%20TPIV.md) drives prey adaptations (radio detection, enhanced hearing)

**Competition:**
- Other rDO-Duskmantle packs (radio conflicts)
- Standard composite predators (vastly outcompeted in darkness)
- No chemical DO competition (cannot function in Dark Forest)

## Sensory Adaptations

### Darkness Specialization

**Echolocation:**
- Active sound pulses (20-80 kHz)
- Echo processing in [SENS brain](Nervous%20Systems.md)
- Three-dimensional terrain mapping
- Prey detection and tracking
- Shared via radio (all partivants "see" echolocation data)

**Infrared Sensing:**
- Pit organs detect heat signatures
- Effective range: 50-100m
- Prey body heat visible in cold Dark Forest
- Integrated with radio positioning

**Minimal Vision:**
- Eyes reduced (some partivants nearly blind)
- Low-light adaptation sufficient for faint [Lumna](Lumna.md) light
- Vision supplementary to other senses
- Energy saved by reducing visual processing

## Comparison to Chemical DOs

### Radio vs Do-Mite Trade-offs

**Radio (rDO-Duskmantle):**
- ✓ Works in complete darkness (CRITICAL for Dark Forest)
- ✓ Longer range (10-15 km)
- ✓ Faster signal speed (instant vs. minutes)
- ✓ Penetrates obstacles
- ✗ Higher energy cost (electrogenesis expensive)
- ✗ Cannot carry nutrients/hormones physically
- ✗ Vulnerable to electromagnetic interference
- ✗ Requires relay nodes (additional partivants)

**Do-Mites ([DO-HillWolf](DO%20HillWolf%20PackHunter.md)):**
- ✓ Lower energy cost
- ✓ Carries physical cargo
- ✓ More secure (chemical specific)
- ✗ Requires light (FATAL in Dark Forest)
- ✗ Limited range (1 km max)
- ✗ Affected by weather
- ✗ Cannot penetrate dense vegetation well

## Threats and Vulnerabilities

### Communication Disruption

**Natural:**
- Electromagnetic storms (very rare on TPIV)
- Mineral deposits (signal absorption in some valleys)
- Terrain interference (deep canyons)

**Biological:**
- Other rDOs jamming frequencies ([radio warfare](DO-Mite%20conflict.md))
- Prey evolving radio-detection capability (early warning)
- Relay node predation (disrupts network)

**Partivant Loss:**
- Cognitive node loss: Coordination collapse
- Relay node loss: Territory fragmentation
- Scout loss: Detection range reduced
- Attack node loss: Hunting success drops
- Can regenerate but slow process

## Research Observations

### Population Density

**Very Low Density:**
- 0.3-1.0 packs per 100 km²
- Lower than chemical DOs (higher territory requirements)
- Dark Forest limited prey density
- Radio range enables sparse distribution

**Pack Composition:**
- 18-30 partivants per pack typical (including relays)
- More partivants than chemical DOs
- Larger territories require more nodes
- Seasonal variation (reproductive partivants added during denning)

**Unique Behavior:**
- Silent coordination (no vocalizations needed)
- Hunting success rate very high (~80% observed)
- Prey detection range exceptional (radio + echolocation)
- Longest documented partivant separation: 15.2 km
